SECTION 1 — FEVER ORIGIN DISCRIMINATION
=====================================================================

Q1. Fever onset?
A:
- “Started suddenly last night.”

Q2. Highest temperature?
A:
- “39.5°C (103°F).”

Q3. Behavior?
A:
- “Not playing normally.”
- “Still playful.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + lethargy
- Fever + poor intake
- Fever >5 days

=====================================================================
SECTION 2 — UPPER RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

COMMON:
- Viral URI
- Influenza
- RSV
- Common cold

CLUES:
- Runny nose
- Cough
- Congestion
- Sore throat

Q:
- “Any nose congestion?”
A:
- “Yes constant.”

RED FLAGS:
- Cyanosis
- Breathing pauses

=====================================================================
SECTION 3 — LOWER RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

PNEUMONIA:
- Fever
- Productive cough or chest pain
- Fast breathing

BRONCHIOLITIS (RSV):
- Wheezing
- Retractions
- Feeding difficulty

Q1. Breathing fast?
A:
- “Yes.”

RED FLAGS:
- Nasal flaring
- Retractions
- Grunting
- Blue lips

=====================================================================
SECTION 4 — EAR INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

ACUTE OTITIS MEDIA:
- Ear pain
- Fever
- Irritability
- Tugging ear

OTITIS EXTERNA:
- Ear canal pain
- Pain with pulling ear

RED FLAGS:
- Mastoid swelling

=====================================================================
SECTION 5 — THROAT INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

VIRAL:
- Cough
- Congestion

STREP:
- Fever
- Sore throat
- No cough
- Swollen glands

RED FLAGS:
- Drooling + neck stiffness
- Difficulty swallowing

=====================================================================
SECTION 6 — GI INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

VIRAL:
- Diarrhea
- Vomiting
- Low-grade fever

BACTERIAL:
- Bloody diarrhea
- Severe abdominal pain

RED FLAGS:
- Bloody diarrhea
- Severe dehydration

=====================================================================
SECTION 7 — URINARY INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Fever without source
- Abdominal pain
- Vomiting
- Strong-smelling urine

Q:
- “Bathroom accidents?”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + vomiting + abdominal pain

=====================================================================
SECTION 8 — SKIN & SOFT TISSUE INFECTIONS
=====================================================================

IMPETIGO:
- Honey crust lesions

CELLULITIS:
- Red
- Warm
- Painful

ABSCESS:
- Fluctuance
- Tender swelling

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid spreading redness
- Fever + swelling

=====================================================================
SECTION 9 — PEDIATRIC SEPSIS
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Fast breathing
- Poor feeding
- Cool extremities
- Lethargic
- ↓ urine output

RED FLAGS:
- ANY lethargy
- ANY mottled skin
- Poor perfusion
- Weak cry

=====================================================================
SECTION 10 — NEONATAL PATTERNS
=====================================================================

0-28 DAYS:
- Hypothermia
- Poor feeding
- Jaundice
- Lethargy
- Irritability

RED FLAGS:
- Fever OR low temperature
- Weak cry

=====================================================================
SECTION 11 — IMMUNE-COMPROMISED KIDS
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Fever with no URI symptoms
- Thrush
- Recurrent infections

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + chemo
- Fever + transplant

=====================================================================
SECTION 12 — GLOBAL PEDIATRIC INFECTION RED FLAGS
=====================================================================
- Breathing difficulty
- Blue lips
- Severe dehydration
- Bloody diarrhea
- Drooling
- Neck stiffness
- Lethargy
- Persistent fever >5 days
- Rash + fever
- Inability to keep fluids
- Seizure with infection

=====================================================================
SECTION 13 — HEALTHYODA PEDIATRIC INFECTION TRIAGE LOGIC
=====================================================================

ASK:
1. Duration of fever?
2. Eating/drinking?
3. Breathing?
4. Urination?
5. Vomiting/diarrhea?
6. Rash?
7. Ear pain?
8. Neck stiffness?
9. Infant <28 days?
10. Immunocompromise?

ROUTE:
- Fever + cough → URI or pneumonia
- Fever + wheezing → bronchiolitis
- Fever + vomiting → GI vs UTI
- Fever + sore throat + no cough → strep
- Fever + lethargy → sepsis
- Fever in neonate → emergency
