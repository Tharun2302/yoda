SECTION 1 — UNEXPLAINED SYNCOPE
=========================================================

Q1. Before fainting, what did you feel?
A:
- "Lightheaded"
- "Tunnel vision"
- "Nothing, just collapsed"

Q2. Triggers?
A:
- "Standing long time"
- "Heat"
- "Pain"

Q3. Heart symptoms?
A: "Heart racing."

SYNCOPE RED FLAGS:
- Occurred during exertion
- Occurred while lying down
- Associated chest pain
- Family history of sudden cardiac death

=========================================================
SECTION 2 — RECURRENT FAINTING
=========================================================

Q1. Frequency?
A: "Once a week."

Q2. Injuries from falls?
A: "Hit head once."

RED FLAGS:
- Seizure features
- Loss of consciousness >2 minutes
- Urinary incontinence with episode

Q1. Swelling duration?
A: "Months."

Q2. Pain?
A: "No pain."

Q3. History?
A:

=========================================================

Q1. Duration?
A: "Weeks of intermittent fever."

Q2. Associated?
A:
- "Night sweats"
- "Weight loss"

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + weight loss
- Fever + lymph nodes
- Fever + rash (vasculitis)

SECTION 5 — UNEXPLAINED BRUISING WITH NORMAL ENERGY
=========================================================

Q1. Any trauma?
A: "No injuries."

Q2. Medications?
A: "No blood thinners."

RED FLAGS:
- Bruising + fever
- Bruising + gum bleeding
- Bruising + fatigue

=========================================================
SECTION 6 — NEUROLOGIC RED-FLAG PATTERNS
=========================================================

Examples:
- Vision loss + headache
- New asymmetric weakness
- New numbness in face + arm/leg
- Difficulty speaking

RED FLAGS:
- Any stroke-like symptom
- Progressive neurologic decline
