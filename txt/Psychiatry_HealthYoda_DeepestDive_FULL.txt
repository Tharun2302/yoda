- "Sudden episode."

- Chronic

- Mild
- Moderate

PATTERN:
- Intermittent episodes
- Constant symptoms
- Triggered by stress

- Sleep issues
- Appetite changes
- Concentration difficulty
- Fatigue
- Palpitations (psych origin)

- Irritability

- Trauma history
- Substance use
- Prior psych conditions
- Family history

SECTION 2 — ANXIETY
=========================================================

Q1. What symptoms do you feel?
A: "Racing thoughts." / "Chest tightness." / "Constant worry."

Q2. Onset?
A: "Started weeks ago."

Q3. Physical symptoms?
A: "Sweaty." / "Shaky." / "Heart races."

Q4. Triggers?
A: "Stress at work." / "No obvious trigger."

Q5. Affecting daily life?
A: "Hard to focus at work."

ANXIETY RED FLAGS:
- Anxiety + chest pain (rule out cardiac)
- Anxiety + fainting
- Anxiety + inability to function

=========================================================

**This section is critical for safety logic (not for diagnosis)**

Q1. Are you having thoughts of hurting yourself?
A: "Yes." / "Sometimes." / "No."

Q2. Do you have a plan?
A: "Yes I have a plan." (RED FLAG)
A: "No plan."

Q3. Intent?
A: "I might act on it." (RED FLAG)

Q4. Past attempts?
A: "Yes in past." (RED FLAG)

SI RED FLAGS (IMMEDIATE ELEVATION):
- SI with plan
- SI with intent
- Recent attempt
- Access to means
- Severe agitation/confusion
