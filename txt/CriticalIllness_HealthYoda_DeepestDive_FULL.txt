SECTION 1 — SEPSIS SPECTRUM (EARLY → SEVERE → SEPTIC SHOCK)
===========================================================

EARLY SEPSIS CLUES:
- Fever or chills
- Body aches
- Cough, UTI symptoms, GI symptoms
- Fast heart rate
- Fatigue

Q1. Recent infection?
A:
- “Had a fever/cough for days.”
- “Pain when peeing.”

Q2. Breathing?
A:
- “Breathing faster than normal.”

SEVERE SEPSIS CLUES:
- Confusion (“not thinking clearly”)
- Very fast breathing
- Low urine output
- Severe weakness

SEPTIC SHOCK CLUES:
- “Feel faint”
- Cold/clammy skin
- Very fast or very weak pulse
- Severe abdominal/chest pain
- Skin mottling

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + confusion
- Fast breathing + weakness
- “Not peeing”
- Any fainting with infection

===========================================================
SECTION 2 — ACUTE RESPIRATORY FAILURE
===========================================================

A. HYPOXIC FAILURE (low oxygen)
CAUSES:
- Pneumonia
- PE
- Pulmonary edema
- ARDS
- Severe asthma

CLUES:
- SOB at rest
- Can't finish sentences
- Blue lips/fingers
- Anxiety

Q1. Describe breathing?
A:
- “Feels like suffocating.”

B. HYPERCAPNIC FAILURE (high CO2)
CAUSES:
- COPD flare
- Neuromuscular weakness
- Drug overdose

CLUES:
- Extreme fatigue
- Confusion
- Sleepiness
- Headache

RED FLAGS:
- Can't speak full sentences
- Blue lips
- Confusion
- Very drowsy

===========================================================
SECTION 3 — METABOLIC CRISIS (DKA, HHS, LACTIC ACID)
===========================================================

A. DKA
CLUES:
- Thirst
- Frequent urination
- Abdominal pain
- Nausea/vomiting
- Fruity breath
- Deep rapid breathing

Q1. Thirst?
A:
- “Extremely thirsty.”

B. HHS
CLUES:
- Severe dehydration
- Confusion
- Lethargy
- High sugar history

C. LACTIC ACIDOSIS
CLUES:
- Rapid breathing
- Severe infection
- Shock signs
- Weakness

RED FLAGS:
- Vomiting + deep breathing
- Confusion + dehydration

===========================================================
SECTION 4 — MULTI-ORGAN DYSFUNCTION SYNDROME (MODS)
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Extreme fatigue
- Confusion
- Jaundice
- No urine output
- Severe SOB
- Cold extremities

Q1. Mental state?
A:
- “Feel out of it.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Blue extremities
- Not urinating

===========================================================
SECTION 5 — SEVERE ELECTROLYTE DERANGEMENTS
===========================================================

A. HYPERKALEMIA
- Palpitations
- Weakness
- Nausea
- History of kidney disease

B. HYPOKALEMIA
- Leg cramps
- Weakness
- Palpitations

C. HYPONATREMIA
- Confusion
- Headache
- Nausea
- Seizure risk

Q1. Mental state?
A:
- “Feeling foggy.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Palpitations
- Seizures

===========================================================
SECTION 6 — ACUTE NEUROLOGIC COLLAPSE
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Sudden weakness/paralysis
- Severe headache
- Confusion
- Seizures
- Fever + neuro symptoms (meningitis)

Q1. Headache?
A:
- “Worst headache ever.”

Q2. Weakness?
A:
- “One side weaker.”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden neurologic deficit
- Seizures
- Confusion + fever

===========================================================
SECTION 7 — ACUTE HEPATIC FAILURE
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Jaundice
- Confusion (hepatic encephalopathy)
- Abdominal swelling
- Nausea/vomiting
- GI bleeding

Q1. Behavior changes?
A:
- “Feeling confused.”

RED FLAGS:
- Jaundice + confusion
- Vomiting blood

===========================================================
SECTION 8 — ACUTE RENAL FAILURE & UREMIC CRISIS
===========================================================

CLUES:
- No urine output
- Swelling
- Confusion
- Nausea
- Itching
- Shortness of breath

Q1. Urination?
A:
- “Barely passed any urine.”

RED FLAGS:
- Not urinating
- Confusion + nausea
- Severe shortness of breath

===========================================================
SECTION 9 — ICU-LEVEL GI EMERGENCIES
===========================================================

A. ACUTE MESENTERIC ISCHEMIA
- Severe abdominal pain “out of proportion”
- Nausea/vomiting
- Blood in stool

B. GI BLEEDING
- Black stools
- Vomiting blood
- Dizziness

C. BOWEL OBSTRUCTION
- Severe abdominal pain
- No gas/stool
- Vomiting

RED FLAGS:
- Severe abdominal pain + faintness
- Blood in vomit/stool

===========================================================
SECTION 10 — IMMUNE DYSREGULATION & CYTOKINE STORM
===========================================================

CAUSES:
- Severe infection
- CAR-T therapy
- Hyperinflammatory states

CLUES:
- High fever
- Extreme fatigue
- Confusion
- Low blood pressure
- Rapid breathing

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + confusion
- Very low energy
- Fast breathing + dizziness

===========================================================
SECTION 11 — GLOBAL CRITICAL ILLNESS RED FLAGS
===========================================================
- Confusion or reduced responsiveness
- Blue lips
- Not breathing normally
- Not peeing
- Severe abdominal/chest pain
- Vomiting blood or black stools
- Severe dehydration
- Seizure
- Sudden paralysis
- Extreme weakness
- Cold/clammy or mottled skin
- Fainting

===========================================================
SECTION 12 — CRITICAL ILLNESS TRIAGE LOGIC
===========================================================

ASK:
1. Infection symptoms?
2. Breathing difficulty?
3. Chest pain?
4. Abdominal pain?
5. Bleeding?
6. Urination?
7. Mental status?
8. Fluid intake ability?
9. Trauma?
10. New medications/exposures?

ROUTE:
- Fever + confusion → sepsis/septic shock  
- Chest pain + weakness → cardiogenic/ischemia  
- Sudden SOB → PE/pneumonia/ARDS  
- Abdominal pain + faintness → ischemia/bleed  
- Nausea/vomiting + deep breathing → DKA  
- Not urinating → renal failure  
- Severe weakness + paralysis → neurologic emergency
