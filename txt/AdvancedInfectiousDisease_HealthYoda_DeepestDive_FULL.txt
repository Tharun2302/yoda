SECTION 1 — FEVER PATTERNS
=============================================================

CONTINUOUS FEVER:
- Typhoid
- Endocarditis
- Pneumonia

INTERMITTENT FEVER:
- Malaria
- Sepsis spikes

RELAPSING FEVER:
- Borrelia
- Tick-borne relapsing fever

Q1. How high is the fever?
A: “102–104°F.”

Q2. Pattern?
A: “Comes and goes every day around evening.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever >104°F
- Fever + confusion
- Fever + stiff neck
- Fever + rash spreading rapidly

=============================================================
SECTION 2 — TRAVEL-RELATED INFECTIONS
=============================================================

REGIONS:
- Africa → malaria, dengue, typhoid
- South Asia → typhoid, dengue, hepatitis
- Latin America → Zika, dengue, Chagas
- Middle East → MERS, brucellosis

Q1. Where did you travel?
A: “Nigeria / India / Brazil / UAE.”

Q2. Mosquito bites?
A: “Many.”

Q3. Water/food exposure?
A: “Street food daily.”

Q4. Symptoms?
A:
- “Fever, chills”
- “Headache”
- “Severe body aches”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever after returning from malaria-endemic region
- Rash + fever + bleeding
- Severe abdominal pain + travel history

=============================================================
SECTION 3 — VECTOR-BORNE DISEASES
=============================================================

TICK-BORNE:
- Lyme: fatigue, joint pain, rash (not always)
- Babesiosis: fever + dark urine
- Anaplasma/Ehrlichia: fever + low WBC + headache

MOSQUITO-BORNE:
- Dengue: severe body pain, retro-orbital pain, bleeding gums
- Zika: rash + conjunctivitis + mild fever
- Malaria: cyclic fevers, chills, sweating

Q1. Any tick bites?
A: “Found tick on leg last week.”

Q2. Rash?
A: “None / circular red rash / small red spots.”

Q3. Severe body aches?
A: “Feels like bones breaking.” (dengue clue)

RED FLAGS:
- Bleeding
- Severe abdominal pain in dengue
- Dark urine + fever (possible hemolysis)

=============================================================
SECTION 4 — ZOONOTIC INFECTIONS
=============================================================

EXPOSURES:
- Cats (Bartonella)
- Dogs (rabies)
- Farm animals (Q fever, brucellosis)
- Rodents (leptospirosis, hantavirus)
- Birds (psittacosis)

Q1. Animal exposures?
A: “Work on farm / bitten / scratched.”

Q2. Symptoms?
A:
- “Fever, chills”
- “Headache”
- “Muscle pain”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + jaundice + red eyes (leptospirosis)
- Fever + severe shortness of breath (hantavirus)
- Neurologic symptoms after animal bite (rabies risk)

=============================================================
SECTION 5 — HEALTHCARE-ASSOCIATED INFECTIONS
=============================================================

COMMON:
- MRSA
- C. diff
- Line infections
- Post-op infections

Q1. Recent hospitalization?
A: “Yes, last month.”

Q2. Diarrhea?
A: “Watery, bad smell.” (C. diff)

Q3. Wound symptoms?
A: “Red, hot, draining fluid.”

RED FLAGS:
- High fever + surgical wound drainage
- Worsening infection with antibiotics
- Bloody diarrhea + dehydration

=============================================================
SECTION 6 — IMMUNOCOMPROMISED INFECTIONS
=============================================================

CAUSES:
- HIV/AIDS
- Chemotherapy
- Chronic steroids
- Transplant patients

COMMON INFECTIONS:
- Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)
- Fungal infections
- CMV
- Disseminated TB

Q1. Immune system issues?
A: “On chemo / transplant meds.”

Q2. Symptoms?
A:
- “Low-grade fever”
- “Shortness of breath”
- “Dry cough”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + neutropenia
- Chest pain + cough in HIV patient
- Severe fungal infection patterns

=============================================================
SECTION 7 — TUBERCULOSIS (TB)
=============================================================

PULMONARY:
- Chronic cough
- Night sweats
- Weight loss
- Fever

EXTRAPULMONARY:
- Lymph nodes
- Spine pain
- Abdominal pain

Q1. Cough duration?
A: “More than 3 weeks.”

Q2. Night sweats?
A: “Soaking sheets.”

RED FLAGS:
- Coughing blood
- Severe weight loss

=============================================================
SECTION 8 — HIV (ACUTE & ADVANCED)
=============================================================

ACUTE INFECTION:
- Fever
- Rash
- Sore throat
- Body aches

ADVANCED:
- Weight loss
- Chronic diarrhea
- Thrush
- Recurrent infections

Q1. When symptoms started?
A: “2–3 weeks after exposure.”

Q2. Rash?
A: “Pink rash on chest.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Severe headache
- Vision changes
- Difficulty breathing

=============================================================
SECTION 9 — HEPATITIS A/B/C
=============================================================

CLUES:
Hep A:
- Nausea
- Fatigue
- Jaundice
- Dark urine

Hep B/C:
- Fatigue
- RUQ pain
- Joint aches (B)
- Chronic liver symptoms (C)

Q1. Urine color?
A: “Dark yellow/brown.”

Q2. Appetite?
A: “Poor.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe abdominal pain
- Confusion (possible liver failure)
- Bleeding

=============================================================
SECTION 10 — CNS INFECTIONS (MENINGITIS / ENCEPHALITIS)
=============================================================

MENINGITIS:
- Fever
- Headache
- Neck stiffness
- Photophobia

ENCEPHALITIS:
- Confusion
- Seizures
- Behavior changes

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + neck stiffness
- Severe headache
- Seizure
- Rapid confusion

=============================================================
SECTION 11 — ENDOCARDITIS
=============================================================

CLUES:
- Fever
- Night sweats
- Fatigue
- New murmur (history-level)
- IV drug use
- Dental work

Q1. Symptoms?
A:
- “Low-grade fever for weeks.”
- “Weight loss.”

RED FLAGS:
- Stroke symptoms
- Shortness of breath
- Chest pain

=============================================================
SECTION 12 — OSTEOMYELITIS / SEPTIC ARTHRITIS
=============================================================

OSTEOMYELITIS:
- Localized bone pain
- Fevers
- Overlying redness

SEPTIC ARTHRITIS:
- Red, hot, swollen joint
- Severe pain
- Trouble moving joint

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + swollen joint
- Red hot knee/hip with inability to walk

=============================================================
SECTION 13 — ABDOMINAL INFECTIONS
=============================================================

APPENDICITIS:
- RLQ pain
- Nausea
- Fever

CHOLANGITIS:
- RUQ pain
- Fever
- Jaundice (Charcot triad)

PERITONITIS:
- Severe abdominal pain
- Guarding/rigidity

RED FLAGS:
- Severe abdominal pain + fever
- Jaundice + fever

=============================================================
SECTION 14 — SKIN / SOFT TISSUE — ADVANCED
=============================================================

CELLULITIS:
- Red, warm, painful skin
- Fever

NECROTIZING FASCIITIS:
- Severe pain out of proportion
- Bullae
- Rapid spread
- Fever

Q1. Pain level?
A: “10/10 but skin doesn’t look that bad.” (nec fasc clue)

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid spreading redness
- Severe pain + fever

=============================================================
SECTION 15 — RESPIRATORY SEVERE INFECTIONS
=============================================================

COVID:
- Fever, cough, SOB
- Loss of smell

INFLUENZA:
- Sudden fever
- Body aches

ATYPICAL PNA:
- Dry cough
- Fatigue
- Headache

RED FLAGS:
- SOB at rest
- Chest pain
- Confusion
- Cyanosis

=============================================================
SECTION 16 — SEPSIS / SEPTIC SHOCK
=============================================================

CLUES:
- Fever or low temp
- Confusion
- Fast breathing
- Fast HR
- Low BP
- Cold extremities

Q1. How do you feel?
A:
- “Very weak.”
- “Confused.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Low blood pressure
- Blue/cold extremities

=============================================================
SECTION 17 — INFECTIOUS DISEASE GLOBAL RED FLAGS
=============================================================
- Fever + confusion
- Fever + rash + rapid spread
- Fever + neck stiffness
- Fever after recent travel
- Red, hot, swollen joint
- Severe abdominal pain + fever
- Shortness of breath + fever
- Coughing blood
- Dark urine + fever after tick bite
- Jaundice + fever
- Seizure with fever
- Shock symptoms (cold, clammy, confused)
