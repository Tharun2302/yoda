SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL PAIN HPI STRUCTURE
=========================================================

Q1. Where is your pain located?
A:
- "All over."
- "Lower back."
- "Neck and shoulders."
- "Hands and feet."
- "Behind one eye."

Q2. When did it start?
A:
- "Started suddenly yesterday."
- "Has been there for months."
- "Years of pain."

Q3. How would you describe the pain?
A:
- "Aching."
- "Sharp or stabbing."
- "Burning."
- "Electric/shooting."
- "Throbbing."

Q4. Intensity (0–10)?
A:
- "Around 3/10 most days."
- "8/10 at worst."

Q5. Constant or comes and goes?
A:
- "Constant dull pain with flares."
- "Comes in attacks."

Q6. What makes it better or worse?
A:
- "Worse with movement."
- "Worse at night."
- "Worse with stress."
- "Better with rest/heat."

Q7. Impact on life?
A:
- "Hard to work."
- "Limits exercise."
- "Sleep disturbed."

PAIN GLOBAL RED FLAGS (FOR ROUTING, NOT DIAGNOSIS):
- Pain with fever and looking ill
- Pain with weight loss or night sweats
- Chest pain with SOB, sweating, or nausea
- Severe abdominal pain with vomiting or rigid abdomen
- Back pain with leg weakness, saddle anesthesia, or incontinence
- New severe headache (“worst headache”) or neuro deficits
- Bone pain with night pain and weight loss
- Pain after major trauma

SECTION 2 — ACUTE VS CHRONIC PAIN DISCRIMINATOR
=========================================================

Q1. How long have you had this pain?
A:
- "Less than a week." (acute)
- "A few weeks." (subacute)
- "More than 3 months." (chronic pain syndrome flag)

Q2. Single event vs long-standing problem?
A:
- "Started after injury."
- "No clear trigger, gradually worse over time."

=========================================================
SECTION 3 — CHRONIC WIDESPREAD PAIN (FIBROMYALGIA-TYPE)
=========================================================

KEY CLUES:
- Pain in multiple body regions
- Chronic duration (≥3 months)
- Non-restorative sleep
- Fatigue and “brain fog”
- Normal neuro exam/history baseline

Q1. Do you have pain in many areas of your body?
A:
- "Yes, neck, shoulders, back, hips, and legs."

Q2. How long has this been going on?
A:
- "For years."

Q3. Do your muscles feel tender to touch?
A:
- "Yes, even light pressure hurts."

Q4. How is your sleep?
A:
- "I sleep but wake up feeling exhausted."

Q5. Any memory or concentration issues (“brain fog”)?
A:
- "Hard to focus or remember things."

Q6. Any major injury or illness right before this started?
A:
- "Started after a stressful period / infection / accident."
- "No obvious trigger."

FIBROMYALGIA-STYLE RED FLAGS (TO LOOK FOR OTHER CAUSES):
- Widespread pain + weight loss
- Widespread pain + fever or night sweats
- Focal neurologic deficits
- Swollen, red joints

SECTION 4 — CHRONIC REGIONAL PAIN (MUSCULOSKELETAL)
=========================================================

Subclusters:
- Neck pain
- Shoulder pain
- Low back pain
- Hip/knee pain

NECK PAIN:
Q1. Where exactly is the pain?
A:
- "At the base of my neck."
- "Radiates to shoulders."

Q2. Numbness or tingling in arms/hands?
A:
- "Tingling down one arm."
- "No numbness."

NECK RED FLAGS:
- Pain after serious trauma
- Neck pain + fever + stiffness
- Neck pain + arm/leg weakness or difficulty walking

SHOULDER PAIN:
Q1. Pain with overhead movement?
A:
- "Worse when lifting arm overhead."

Q2. Night pain lying on shoulder?
A:
- "Cannot sleep on that side."

BACK PAIN (CHRONIC):
Q1. Where is the back pain?
A:
- "Across lower back."
- "One side into buttock."

Q2. Does the pain travel down your leg?
A:
- "Yes to below the knee."
- "Stays in the back."

Q3. Any numbness, tingling, or weakness?
A:
- "Tingling in foot."
- "No weakness that I notice."

BACK PAIN RED FLAGS:
- Back pain with fever or chills
- Back pain with weight loss and history of cancer
- Back pain with leg weakness, saddle numbness, or bladder/bowel dysfunction
- Severe nighttime back pain unrelieved by rest

KNEE/HIP PAIN (CHRONIC):
Q1. Worse with walking/using stairs?
A:
- "Worse going downstairs."
Q2. Morning stiffness duration?
A:
- "Stiff for less than 30 minutes." (degenerative pattern)
- "Stiff for over an hour." (inflammatory pattern flag)

=========================================================
SECTION 5 — NEUROPATHIC PAIN
=========================================================

CHARACTER:
- Burning
- Electric/shooting
- Tingling
- Numbness with pain

COMMON PATTERNS:
- Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
- Post-herpetic neuralgia
- Radiculopathy
- Entrapment neuropathies (carpal tunnel, etc.)

Q1. How would you describe the pain?
A:
- "Feels like burning."
- "Like electric shocks."

Q2. Location and pattern?
A:
- "Both feet, like wearing tight socks."
- "Down the arm into the hand."

Q3. Numbness or tingling?
A:
- "Toes feel numb."
- "Pins and needles."

Q4. Worse at night?
A:
- "Yes, keeps me awake."

NEUROPATHIC RED FLAGS:
- Rapidly progressive weakness
- Sudden onset with stroke-like symptoms
- Neuropathic pain with bladder/bowel issues

=========================================================
SECTION 6 — COMPLEX REGIONAL PAIN SYNDROME (CRPS-LIKE)
=========================================================

CORE FEATURES:
- Severe, disproportionate limb pain
- Swelling, color changes
- Temperature changes, sweating asymmetry
- Allodynia (pain from light touch)

Q1. Did your pain start after an injury or surgery to that limb?
A:
- "Yes, after a wrist fracture."
- "After ankle surgery."

Q2. How would you describe the pain?
A:
- "Burning and constant."
- "Even light touch or air hurts."

Q3. Any swelling, color, or temperature changes?
A:
- "Hand looks red and sometimes purple."
- "That foot feels much colder than the other."

Q4. Any change in hair/nail growth on that limb?
A:
- "Yes, hair seems different and skin is shiny."

CRPS RED FLAGS:
- Severe limb pain out of proportion to injury
- Progressive loss of function
- Severe allodynia with swelling and color changes

SECTION 7 — CHRONIC HEADACHE SYNDROMES (PAIN VIEW)
=========================================================

MIGRAINE-LIKE:
Q1. Location?
A:
- "One side of head, throbbing."

Q2. Associated with?
A:
- "Nausea, sensitive to light and sound."

Q3. Duration?
A:
- "Lasts hours to a full day."

TENSION-TYPE:
A:
- "Band-like pressure on both sides."

CLUSTER-TYPE:
A:
- "Severe pain around one eye."
- "Eye tears/red, nose runs."
- "Comes in short, very intense attacks."

HEADACHE RED FLAGS:
- Sudden “worst headache of my life”
- Headache with fever and stiff neck
- Headache with neurologic symptoms (weakness, confusion, vision loss)
- New headache in older patient or with cancer history

SECTION 8 — CHRONIC ABDOMINAL PAIN (FUNCTIONAL VS ORGANIC FLAGS)
=========================================================

Q1. Duration?
A:
- "Most days for 6 months."

Q2. Relation to food or bowel movements?
A:
- "Better after bowel movement."
- "Worse after certain foods."

Q3. Red-flag GI symptoms?
A:
- "Unintended weight loss."
- "Blood in stool."
- "Waking at night with pain."

ABDOMINAL PAIN RED FLAGS:
- Persistent vomiting
- GI bleeding (red or black stool)
- Weight loss
- Severe localized pain with fever

SECTION 9 — CHRONIC PELVIC PAIN
=========================================================

Q1. Where is your pain?
A:
- "Low in the pelvis."
- "Deep in the vagina with intercourse."

Q2. Relation to menstrual cycle?
A:
- "Worse during periods."
- "Mid-cycle pain."

Q3. Bowel or bladder triggers?
A:
- "Worse when bladder is full."
- "Worse with bowel movements."

PELVIC PAIN RED FLAGS:
- Pelvic pain with fever
- Pain with heavy bleeding or pregnancy
- Pelvic pain with unexplained weight loss

=========================================================
SECTION 10 — CENTRAL SENSITIZATION / AMPLIFIED PAIN
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Pain out of proportion to exam
- Multiple pain sites
- Heightened sensitivity to touch, sound, or light
- Long history of pain clinic/ER visits

Q1. How sensitive are you to touch or pressure?
A:
- "Even light touch hurts."

Q2. Other sensitivity?
A:
- "Very sensitive to loud sounds or bright light."

Q3. History of many tests?
A:
- "I've had many tests and no cause found."

RED FLAGS (SEARCH FOR ORGANIC CAUSES):
- New or rapidly changing pain
- Neurologic symptoms
- Fever/weight loss

SECTION 11 — CANCER-RELATED PAIN (SYMBOLIC PATTERN ONLY)
=========================================================

Q1. Any history of cancer?
A:
- "Yes, treated for cancer in the past."

Q2. Pain location?
A:
- "Bone pain, worse at night."
- "New persistent pain near tumor site."

RED FLAGS:
- Night pain that wakes patient
- Bone pain + weight loss
- Pain with pathologic fractures

=========================================================
SECTION 13 — OPIOID-RELATED PAIN ISSUES
=========================================================

Q1. Are you taking opioids for pain?
A:
- "Yes, daily."

Q2. Does pain seem more sensitive or widespread over time?
A:
- "Yes, smaller things hurt more now."

OPIOID RED FLAGS:
- Escalating doses
- Using more than prescribed
- Overdose symptoms (confusion, slow breathing)
