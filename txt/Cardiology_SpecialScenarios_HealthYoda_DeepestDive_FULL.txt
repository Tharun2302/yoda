SECTION 1 — MYOCARDITIS
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Chest pain (often sharp, dull, or pressure)
- Recent viral illness
- Palpitations
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Worse with exertion

Q1. Recent infection?
A:
- “Had flu-like illness last week.”

Q2. Chest pain?
A:
- “Dull chest ache, gets worse with activity.”

Q3. Palpitations?
A:
- “Heart racing suddenly.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fainting
- Severe shortness of breath
- Rapid heart rate with dizziness

==============================================================
SECTION 2 — PERICARDITIS
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Sharp chest pain
- Worse lying flat
- Better sitting up / leaning forward
- May follow viral illness
- Pain may radiate to shoulders

Q1. What makes pain better or worse?
A:
- “Worse when lying down; better when sitting up.”

Q2. Fever?
A:
- “Low fever for days.”

RED FLAGS:
- Increasing SOB
- Low blood pressure
- Lightheadedness (possible evolving tamponade)

==============================================================
SECTION 3 — PERICARDIAL EFFUSION → TAMPONADE
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Shortness of breath
- Chest pressure
- Difficulty lying flat
- Lightheadedness
- History of cancer, infection, or autoimmune condition

Q1. Breathing?
A:
- “Hard to breathe when lying flat.”

Q2. Energy levels?
A:
- “Very weak and dizzy.”

RED FLAGS:
- Low BP
- Fast HR
- Confusion (shock)

==============================================================
SECTION 4 — AORTIC DISSECTION
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Sudden, severe chest or back pain
- Tearing/ripping sensation
- Migrating pain
- Unequal pulses
- History of hypertension or connective tissue disorders

Q1. Describe the pain?
A:
- “Sudden tearing pain in chest going to my back.”

Q2. Any neurologic symptoms?
A:
- “Weakness in one leg.”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden severe chest/back pain
- Fainting
- Speech/vision changes

==============================================================
SECTION 5 — PULMONARY HYPERTENSION
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Progressive shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Edema
- Chest discomfort
- Syncope with exertion

Q1. SOB timeline?
A:
- “Getting worse over months.”

Q2. Chest symptoms?
A:
- “Tightness with exertion.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fainting with exertion
- Blue lips
- Severe swelling

==============================================================
SECTION 6 — HEART FAILURE EXACERBATION
==============================================================

ACUTE DECOMPENSATED HF:
- Sudden SOB
- Waking up at night gasping
- Leg swelling
- Weight gain
- Fatigue

Q1. Weight gain?
A:
- “5 pounds in a few days.”

Q2. Waking up breathless?
A:
- “Yes, need to sit up.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe SOB at rest
- Chest pain
- Pink frothy sputum

==============================================================
SECTION 7 — CARDIOGENIC SHOCK
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Severe SOB
- Cold/clammy skin
- Confusion
- Low urine output
- Chest pain history or severe CHF

Q1. How do you feel?
A:
- “Very weak, dizzy.”

RED FLAGS:
- Low BP
- Confusion
- Blue lips/fingers

==============================================================
SECTION 8 — ARRHYTHMIAS
==============================================================

AFIB WITH RVR:
- Palpitations
- SOB
- Dizziness
- Fatigue

SVT:
- Sudden racing heart
- Chest tightness
- Lightheadedness

VT:
- Palpitations + near-syncope
- History of heart disease

BRADYCARDIA:
- Fatigue
- Dizziness
- Fainting

RED FLAGS:
- Syncope
- Chest pain + palpitations
- Pale/sweaty appearance

==============================================================
SECTION 9 — CARDIAC SYNCOPE RED-FLAG PATTERNS
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Fainting during exertion
- Fainting without warning
- Palpitations before fainting
- Family history of sudden cardiac death

Q1. What were you doing when you fainted?
A:
- “Exercising.”

RED FLAGS:
- Exertional syncope
- Chest pain before fainting
- Family history of sudden death

==============================================================
SECTION 10 — HYPERTROPHIC CARDIOMYOPATHY (HCM)
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Family history of sudden death
- Exertional chest pain
- Exertional syncope
- Palpitations

Q1. Family history?
A:
- “Yes, uncle died suddenly young.”

RED FLAGS:
- Syncope with exercise
- Sudden chest pain

==============================================================
SECTION 11 — TAKOTSUBO (STRESS CARDIOMYOPATHY)
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Severe emotional stress
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath
- Often post-menopausal woman

Q1. Recent stress?
A:
- “Lost a loved one.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe SOB
- Persistent chest pain

==============================================================
SECTION 12 — ADVANCED ENDOCARDITIS PATTERNS
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Fever for weeks
- Night sweats
- Fatigue
- New murmur (history-level)
- IVDU
- Stroke symptoms

Q1. Symptoms?
A:
- “Weak for weeks, sweating at night.”

Q2. Dental work?
A:
- “Had dental work recently.”

RED FLAGS:
- Stroke symptoms
- Severe headache
- SOB

==============================================================
SECTION 13 — ATYPICAL ISCHEMIA PRESENTATIONS
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Epigastric pain
- Jaw pain
- Back pain
- Fatigue
- No classic chest pain
- Common in women, elderly, diabetics

Q1. What is the discomfort like?
A:
- “Pressure in upper stomach.”
- “Jaw aches.”

RED FLAGS:
- SOB + nausea
- Sudden fatigue + sweating

==============================================================
SECTION 14 — AORTIC STENOSIS SYMPTOMS
==============================================================

TRIAD:
- Exertional chest pain
- Exertional syncope
- Exertional shortness of breath

Q1. Exercise symptoms?
A:
- “Feel dizzy when climbing stairs.”

RED FLAGS:
- Syncope during exertion
- Chest pain with exertion

==============================================================
SECTION 15 — ADULT CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
==============================================================

CLUES:
- Cyanosis
- Clubbing
- Exercise intolerance
- Palpitations
- Murmur history

Q1. Childhood heart problems?
A:
- “Had heart surgery as a baby.”

RED FLAGS:
- Blue lips
- Fainting

==============================================================
SECTION 16 — CARDIO-ONCOLOGY COMPLICATIONS
==============================================================

CHEMO-RELATED:
- SOB
- Fatigue
- Chest pain
- Edema

Q1. Recent chemotherapy?
A:
- “Yes, on anthracycline.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe SOB
- New chest pain

==============================================================
SECTION 17 — GLOBAL CARDIOLOGY RED FLAGS
==============================================================
- Chest pain with SOB, sweating, nausea
- Chest pain radiating to jaw/arm/back
- Sudden tearing chest or back pain
- Palpitations + dizziness or fainting
- Syncope during exercise
- Blue lips, cyanosis
- Rapid swelling + SOB
- Pink frothy sputum
- New confusion with low blood pressure
- Severe tachycardia or bradycardia
- History of cancer + new SOB/chest pain
- Fever + murmur + weight loss (endocarditis)
