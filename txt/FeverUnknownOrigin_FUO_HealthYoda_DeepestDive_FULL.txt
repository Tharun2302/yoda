SECTION 1 — INFECTIOUS FUO (SLOW-BURN)
=================================================================

COMMON CULPRITS:
- Tuberculosis (esp. extrapulmonary)
- Endocarditis (subacute)
- Abscesses (liver, spleen, pelvic, dental)
- Osteomyelitis
- Lyme disease
- Viral infections (EBV, CMV, HIV acute/chronic)
- Fungal illnesses

CLUES:
- Night sweats
- Weight loss
- Fatigue
- Chronic cough or chest discomfort
- Joint aches
- Localized pain (spine, abdomen)

Q1. Night sweats?
A:
- “Drenching sweats at night.”

Q2. Weight loss?
A:
- “Lost 10 lbs unintentionally.”

Q3. Travel or animal exposures?
A:
- “Visited farm / caves / rural area.”

Q4. Dental issues?
A:
- “Recent dental work.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + night sweats + weight loss  
- Fever + new heart murmur history-level  
- Fever + bone/back pain (osteomyelitis)  
- Persistent fever >3 weeks  

=================================================================
SECTION 2 — AUTOIMMUNE / INFLAMMATORY FUO
=================================================================

COMMON CAUSES:
- Still’s disease
- Systemic lupus (SLE)
- Vasculitis
- Sarcoidosis
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- IBD (flare)
- Adult-onset Still’s disease (quotidian fever pattern)

CLUES:
- Rash (salmon-colored)
- Joint swelling
- Morning stiffness
- Dry eyes/mouth
- Painful swollen lymph nodes
- Chest pain with breathing (serositis)

Q1. Rashes?
A:
- “Faint pink rash that comes and goes.”

Q2. Joint symptoms?
A:
- “Morning stiffness for hours.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + rash + joint pain  
- Fever + visual symptoms  
- Fever + chest pain + SOB (serositis)  

=================================================================
SECTION 3 — MALIGNANCY-RELATED FUO
=================================================================

COMMON CAUSES:
- Lymphoma
- Leukemia
- Solid tumor fever
- Paraneoplastic fevers

CLUES:
- Night sweats
- Weight loss
- Fatigue
- Enlarging lymph nodes
- Bone pain
- Easy bruising

Q1. Lymph nodes?
A:
- “Neck/groin nodes feel swollen.”

Q2. Bruising?
A:
- “Bruise easily.”

Q3. Bone pain?
A:
- “Deep aching pain at night.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + weight loss + night sweats  
- Bruising + persistent fever  
- Progressive fatigue + swollen nodes  

=================================================================
SECTION 4 — MISCELLANEOUS FUO (ENDOCRINE / METABOLIC)
=================================================================

CAUSES:
- Thyroid storm
- Adrenal insufficiency
- Hyperthyroidism
- Hyper-IgG diseases
- Familial Mediterranean Fever (FMF)

CLUES:
- Heat intolerance
- Sweating
- Dizziness
- Hair loss
- Recurrent abdominal/chest pain episodes (FMF)

Q1. Temperature intolerance?
A:
- “Feel hot all the time.”

Q2. Recurrent abdominal episodes?
A:
- “Painful flare every few weeks.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + confusion + fast HR (thyroid storm)  
- Persistent low BP with fever (adrenal issue)  

=================================================================
SECTION 5 — DRUG-INDUCED FEVER
=================================================================

CAUSES:
- Antibiotics (beta-lactams)
- Anticonvulsants
- Allopurinol
- Heparin
- Antidepressants
- Sedatives
- IL inhibitors

CLUES:
- Fever appears days–weeks after starting medication
- Rash
- Lymph node swelling
- Facial swelling (DRESS syndrome)
- No localizing symptoms

Q1. New medications?
A:
- “Started new antibiotic.”

Q2. Rash?
A:
- “Red rash on body.”

RED FLAGS:
- Rash + fever + facial swelling  
- Fever + confusion after med change  

=================================================================
SECTION 6 — EXPOSURE-RELATED FUO
=================================================================

CAUSES:
- Travel infections
- Tick-borne: Lyme, babesiosis, ehrlichiosis
- Malaria
- Brucellosis
- Leptospirosis
- Q fever

Q1. Travel?
A:
- “Returned from Africa/Asia/South America.”

Q2. Mosquito/tick bites?
A:
- “Yes, hiking recently.”

Q3. Animals?
A:
- “Work with livestock.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + travel + severe headache  
- Fever + jaundice + vomiting  
- Fever + dark urine

SECTION 7 — FUO IN IMMUNOCOMPROMISED PATIENTS
=================================================================

CAUSES:
- Opportunistic infections
- Fungal infections
- Viral reactivation (CMV)
- Pneumocystis (PCP)
- Catheter infections
- Drug fevers

CLUES:
- Cough
- SOB
- Oral thrush
- Pain at catheter site
- Chronic diarrhea

Q1. Immune status?
A:
- “On chemo.”
- “On steroids.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + neutropenia symptoms  
- Fever + confusion  
- SOB + immune suppression

SECTION 8 — FUO IN RETURNING TRAVELERS
=================================================================

REGIONS → SYMPTOM CLUSTERS:
- AFRICA: malaria, dengue, brucellosis
- SOUTH AMERICA: dengue, leishmaniasis
- ASIA: typhoid, scrub typhus
- MIDDLE EAST: Q fever, brucellosis

Q1. Where did you travel?
A:
- “Tanzania / India / Brazil.”

Q2. Water/food exposures?
A:
- “Ate from street vendors.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + jaundice  
- Fever + severe headache  
- Fever + bleeding tendency  

=================================================================
SECTION 9 — FUO IN CHILDREN
=================================================================

CAUSES:
- Viral infections (most common)
- Kawasaki disease
- Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (JIA)
- Autoimmune disease
- Hidden abscesses

CLUES:
- Rash
- Red eyes
- Strawberry tongue
- Swollen glands

RED FLAGS:
- Fever >5 days + rash  
- Red eyes + cracked lips (Kawasaki)  
- Dehydration + no urine  

=================================================================
SECTION 10 — GLOBAL FUO RED FLAGS
=================================================================
- Fever lasting more than 3 weeks  
- Night sweats  
- Weight loss  
- Severe headache  
- Confusion  
- Coughing blood  
- Dark urine or jaundice  
- Chest pain  
- Black stools  
- Severe abdominal pain  
- Shortness of breath  
- Rapidly enlarging lymph nodes

SECTION 11 — FUO TRIAGE LOGIC (FOR HEALTHYODA)
=================================================================

ASK:
1. Weight loss?
2. Night sweats?
3. Travel?
4. Animal exposures?
5. Lymph node swelling?
6. Cough?
7. Abdominal pain?
8. Urinary symptoms?
9. Rash?
10. New medications?
11. Immune system issues?
12. Bleeding/bruising?
13. Headache/neurologic symptoms?

ROUTE:
- Weight loss + night sweats → TB / lymphoma  
- Travel + fever → malaria/dengue  
- Rash + meds → drug fever  
- Rash + red eyes → Kawasaki (children)  
- Fever + chest pain → pericarditis/PE/infection  
- Fever + confusion → sepsis/meningitis  
- Fever + abdominal pain → abscess, FMF, IBD  
- Fever + lymph nodes → malignancy/infection  
- Fever + cough → pneumonia/TB  
- Fever + dark urine → malaria/hepatitis
