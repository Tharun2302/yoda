SECTION 1 — FRAILTY
====================================================================

CLUES:
- Low strength
- Slow walking
- Exhaustion
- Weight loss
- Frequent illness
- Reduced activity

Q1. Energy?
A:
- “Feel weak and tired all day.”

Q2. Walking?
A:
- “Slow, need to stop often.”

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid functional decline  
- Weight loss without trying  

====================================================================
SECTION 2 — FALLS
====================================================================

COMMON CAUSES:
- Gait instability
- Weakness
- Medication effects
- Dehydration
- Vision problems
- Neurologic disease

Q1. Why did you fall?
A:
- “Lost balance.”

Q2. How many falls?
A:
- “Three this month.”

RED FLAGS:
- Falls + head injury  
- Falls + anticoagulants  

====================================================================
SECTION 3 — DELIRIUM (ACUTE CONFUSION)
====================================================================

CLUES:
- Sudden confusion
- Fluctuates day to day
- Hallucinations
- Sleep-wake cycle change

Q1. When did confusion start?
A:
- “Suddenly yesterday.”

COMMON TRIGGERS:
- Infection
- Medications
- Dehydration
- Pain
- Hospitalization

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + confusion  
- Inability to wake  
- New hallucinations  

====================================================================
SECTION 4 — DEMENTIA
====================================================================

Alzheimer’s:
- Memory loss first
- Repetition
- Word finding issues

Vascular:
- Stepwise decline
- Stroke history

Lewy Body:
- Fluctuating alertness
- Hallucinations
- Parkinson-like

Frontotemporal:
- Personality change
- Behavior issues

Q:
- “What changed recently?”
A:
- “Memory slipping over months.”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden major change  
- Difficulty swallowing  

====================================================================
SECTION 5 — POLYPHARMACY
====================================================================

High-risk indicators:
- >5 medications
- Sedatives
- Sleeping pills
- Anticholinergics
- Opioids
- Benzos

Q1. Any new medications?
A:
- “Started two new pills.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion after med change  
- Falls after med change  

====================================================================
SECTION 6 — URINARY INCONTINENCE
====================================================================

STRESS:
- Leakage with cough/laugh

URGE:
- Sudden urge

OVERFLOW:
- Dribbling
- Weak stream

FUNCTIONAL:
- Can’t reach toilet

Q1. When do leaks happen?
A:
- “When coughing.”

RED FLAGS:
- Blood in urine  
- Fever + abdominal pain  

====================================================================
SECTION 7 — CONSTIPATION
====================================================================

CLUES:
- Hard stool
- Straining
- Inability to evacuate
- Pain
- Decreased appetite

COMMON CAUSES:
- Medications
- Dehydration
- Immobility

RED FLAGS:
- Constipation + vomiting  
- Black stools  

====================================================================
SECTION 8 — MALNUTRITION & WEIGHT LOSS
====================================================================

CAUSES:
- Depression
- Dementia
- Oral pain
- Poor access
- Medication effects

Q1. Appetite?
A:
- “Eating less lately.”

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid weight loss  
- Difficulty swallowing  

====================================================================
SECTION 9 — PAIN PRESENTATIONS
====================================================================

Elder pain differs:
- Vague complaints
- Fatigue
- Functional decline

Common causes:
- Osteoarthritis
- Neuropathic pain
- Spinal stenosis

RED FLAGS:
- Severe back pain + weakness  
- Pain + confusion  

====================================================================
SECTION 10 — DEPRESSION IN ELDERLY
====================================================================

CLUES:
- Low interest
- Sleep change
- Appetite change
- Withdrawal

Often misdiagnosed as dementia

Q1. Mood?
A:
- “Feel sad most days.”

RED FLAGS:
- Suicidal thoughts  

====================================================================

====================================================================

CAUSES:

- Pain
- Anxiety
- Urinary frequency
- Sleep apnea

Q:
- “Up often at night?”

A:
- “Yes, several times.”

SECTION 12 — SKIN BREAKDOWN
====================================================================

CLUES:

- Breakdown

Risk factors:
- Immobility
- Incontinence
- Malnutrition

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid spreading redness  
- Fever

====================================================================
SECTION 14 — GLOBAL GERIATRIC RED FLAGS
====================================================================
- Sudden confusion  
- Falls + head injury  
- Rapid decline  
- Fever + confusion  
- Black/tarry stools  
- Blood in urine  
- Inability to stand  
- Inability to eat or drink  
- Severe dehydration  
- New severe pain  
- Suicidal thoughts  

====================================================================
SECTION 15 — GERIATRIC TRIAGE LOGIC (for HealthYoda)
====================================================================

ASK:
1. Any falls recently?
2. Confusion sudden or gradual?
3. Appetite / weight?
4. Pain anywhere?
5. Med changes?
6. Ability to walk?
7. Urinary symptoms?
8. Mood?
9. Sleep?
10. Support at home?

ROUTE:
- Sudden confusion → delirium  
- Falls + meds → polypharmacy  
- Weight loss + withdrawal → depression/neglect  
- Urinary urgency + incontinence → urge type  
- Weakness + falls → frailty  
- Pressure pain + immobility → skin breakdown risk
