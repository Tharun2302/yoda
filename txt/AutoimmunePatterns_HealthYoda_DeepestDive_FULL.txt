SECTION 1 — SYSTEMIC AUTOIMMUNE “BIG 5” PATTERN
=====================================================

Consider SLE, RA, Sjögren’s, Scleroderma, Mixed CTD.

Q1. Fatigue?
A:
- “Severe tiredness I can’t explain.”

Q2. Joint symptoms?
A:
- “Aching in hands and wrists.”
- “Stiff in the morning for over an hour.”

Q3. Rashes?
A:
- “Red rash on cheeks after sun.”

Q4. Mouth/eye dryness?
A:
- “Hard to swallow dry foods.”

Q5. Raynaud’s?
A:
- “Fingers turn white/blue in cold.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + rash + joint pain  
- SOB + chest pain (serositis)  
- Leg swelling + foamy urine (renal disease)  

=====================================================
SECTION 2 — SYSTEMIC LUPUS ERYTHEMATOSUS (SLE)
=====================================================

CLUES:
- Malar rash, photosensitivity
- Non-erosive joint pain
- Mouth/nasal ulcers
- Pleuritic chest pain
- Hair loss
- Fevers
- Raynaud’s
- Kidney involvement (frothy urine, swelling)

Q1. Rashes?
A:
- “Butterfly rash on cheeks that worsens in sun.”

Q2. Chest pain?
A:
- “Sharp pain when breathing deeply.”

Q3. Urine changes?
A:
- “Foamy urine.”

RED FLAGS:
- Chest pain + SOB → possible pericarditis/pleuritis  
- Confusion or severe headache  
- Facial swelling + decreased urination  

=====================================================
SECTION 3 — SJÖGREN’S SYNDROME
=====================================================

CLUES:
- Dry eyes (“gritty feeling”)
- Dry mouth (dental issues)
- Parotid swelling
- Fatigue
- Arthralgias

Q1. Eye symptoms?
A:
- “Feels like sand in eyes.”

Q2. Mouth?
A:
- “Need water to swallow bread.”

RED FLAGS:
- Persistent parotid swelling  
- Severe dental decay  

=====================================================
SECTION 4 — SYSTEMIC SCLEROSIS (SCLERODERMA)
=====================================================

CLUES:
- Skin thickening/tightness
- Finger puffiness
- Raynaud’s
- Heartburn, swallowing problems
- SOB (pulmonary hypertension or fibrosis)

Q1. Skin changes?
A:
- “Skin on fingers feels tight.”

Q2. Heartburn?
A:
- “Constant reflux, trouble swallowing.”

RED FLAGS:
- SOB + scleroderma → possible pulmonary hypertension  
- Severe dysphagia/food impaction  

=====================================================
SECTION 5 — MIXED CONNECTIVE TISSUE DISEASE (MCTD)
=====================================================

CLUES:
- Raynaud’s
- Puffy fingers
- Joint pain
- Myositis-like weakness
- Features of SLE + scleroderma + polymyositis

Q1. Finger swelling?
A:
- “Whole fingers feel swollen.”

RED FLAGS:
- SOB  
- New muscle weakness  

=====================================================
SECTION 6 — VASCULITIS SPECTRUM
=====================================================

TYPES (high-level HPI):
- ANCA-associated (sinus, lung, kidney)
- PAN (abdominal pain + neuropathy)
- HSP (purpura + abdominal pain)
- GCA (headache, jaw pain)
- Behçet’s (oral/genital ulcers)

GENERAL CLUES:
- Purpuric rash
- Abdominal pain
- Neuropathy
- Sinus symptoms
- Coughing blood
- Kidney issues (dark urine)

Q1. Rashes?
A:
- “Red spots on legs that don’t fade.”

Q2. Sinus issues?
A:
- “Chronic congestion and nosebleeds.”

Q3. Nerve symptoms?
A:
- “Foot numbness.”

RED FLAGS:
- Eye pain + headache (GCA)  
- Coughing blood  
- Dark urine + swelling  

=====================================================
SECTION 7 — INFLAMMATORY MYOPATHIES
=====================================================

Polymyositis / Dermatomyositis

CLUES:
- Proximal muscle weakness (stairs, lifting arms)
- Difficulty combing hair
- Fatigue
- Rash (heliotrope, Gottron’s papules)

Q1. Weakness?
A:
- “Can’t lift arms to brush hair.”

Q2. Rash?
A:
- “Purple rash on eyelids.”

RED FLAGS:
- Difficulty swallowing  
- Difficulty breathing  

=====================================================
SECTION 8 — AUTOIMMUNE THYROID DISEASE
=====================================================

HASHIMOTO’S:
- Fatigue
- Weight gain
- Cold intolerance
- Hair loss
- Constipation

GRAVES’:
- Heat intolerance
- Anxiety
- Palpitations
- Weight loss
- Eye bulging

Q1. Temperature tolerance?
A:
- “Always cold / always hot.”

Q2. Heart symptoms?
A:
- “Heart races.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe palpitations  
- Eye pain + vision changes  

=====================================================
SECTION 9 — AUTOIMMUNE GI (IBD EXTRA-INTESTINAL)
=====================================================

CONSIDER:
- Crohn’s disease
- Ulcerative colitis

EXTRA-INTESTINAL:
- Joint pain
- Eye inflammation (uveitis)
- Skin lesions (erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum)
- Fatigue

Q1. GI symptoms?
A:
- “Chronic diarrhea.”
- “Bloody stools.”

Q2. Eye symptoms?
A:
- “Red painful eye.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe abdominal pain  
- High fever  
- Visual symptoms  

=====================================================
SECTION 10 — AUTOIMMUNE LIVER & BILIARY
=====================================================

TYPES:
- Autoimmune hepatitis (AIH)
- Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC)
- Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC)

CLUES:
- RUQ discomfort
- Itching
- Fatigue
- Jaundice
- Dark urine

Q1. Skin itching?
A:
- “Itching all over.”

Q2. Urine color?
A:
- “Dark.”

RED FLAGS:
- Jaundice + confusion (liver failure)  
- Severe abdominal pain  

=====================================================
SECTION 11 — AUTOIMMUNE NEUROLOGICAL DISORDERS
=====================================================

PERIPHERAL:
- Guillain-Barré: ascending weakness
- CIDP: chronic version
- Myasthenia gravis: fatigue with repetition

CENTRAL:
- MS: vision changes, limb tingling, episodes

Q1. Weakness?
A:
- “Legs getting weaker.”

Q2. Vision problems?
A:
- “Blurry vision in one eye.”

RED FLAGS:
- Rapidly rising weakness  
- Trouble breathing  
- Sudden loss of vision  

=====================================================
SECTION 12 — AUTOIMMUNE SKIN DISEASES
=====================================================

PSORIASIS:
- Red patches + silvery scale
- Nail pitting

DERMATITIS HERPETIFORMIS:
- Clusters of itchy blisters (celiac-associated)

VITILIGO:
- Depigmented patches

LUPUS SKIN:
- Photosensitive rash

Q1. Describe the rash?
A:
- “Red scaly patches.”
- “Very itchy clusters.”

RED FLAGS:
- Rash + fever  
- Widespread blistering  

=====================================================
SECTION 13 — MULTI-SYSTEM AUTOIMMUNE FLARE LOGIC
=====================================================

KEY QUESTIONS:
1. Fever?
2. Weight loss?
3. Night sweats?
4. SOB or chest pain?
5. Joint swelling?
6. Rash?
7. Mouth/eye dryness?
8. Weakness?
9. New numbness?
10. GI or urinary changes?

FLARE RED-FLAG OUTPUT:
- SOB + chest pain (serositis)  
- Kidney involvement (foamy urine, swelling)  
- Progressive weakness  
- Severe headache or confusion  
- High fever + rash + joint pain  

=====================================================
SECTION 14 — AUTOIMMUNE EMERGENCY RED FLAGS (CONSOLIDATED)
=====================================================
- Vision loss or severe eye pain  
- Chest pain with deep breathing  
- Shortness of breath  
- Sudden weakness or paralysis  
- High fever + rash  
- Dark urine + swelling  
- Unintentional weight loss >10 lbs  
- Severe abdominal pain  
- Difficulty swallowing or breathing (myositis/MG)
