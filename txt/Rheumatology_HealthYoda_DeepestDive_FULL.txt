SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL RHEUMATOLOGY HPI STRUCTURE
=========================================================

Q1. Where are your symptoms?
A:
- "Knees."
- "Hands and wrists."
- "Lower back."
- "Multiple joints."

Q2. Onset?
A:
- "Sudden overnight."
- "Gradual over months."

Q3. Morning stiffness?
A:
- "Lasts 1–2 hours." (inflammatory)
- "Stiff for 5–10 minutes." (mechanical/degenerative)

Q4. Pattern?
A:
- "Both sides of body." (symmetric)
- "One knee and one toe." (asymmetric)

Q5. Swelling?
A:
- "Joints look puffy."
- "Red, hot, painful joint."

Q6. Triggers?
A:
- "Injury."
- "Cold weather."
- "After infection."

Q7. Systemic symptoms?
A:
- "Rash."
- "Fever."
- "Weight loss."
- "Dry eyes/mouth."

RHEUM GLOBAL RED FLAGS:
- Red, hot, intensely painful joint (possible septic joint or gout)
- Joint pain + fever + chills
- Rash + fever + joint pain
- Night sweats + weight loss + swollen nodes
- Back pain + bowel/bladder issues
- New severe muscle weakness

SECTION 2 — INFLAMMATORY VS MECHANICAL PAIN DISCRIMINATOR
=========================================================

INFLAMMATORY:
- Morning stiffness > 45–60 minutes
- Better with movement
- Worse with rest
- Swelling/warmth

MECHANICAL:
- Morning stiffness < 10 minutes
- Worse as day goes on
- Worse with activity
- Improves with rest

Q1. How long does stiffness last in the morning?
A:
- "About an hour." (inflammatory)
- "Only 5 minutes." (mechanical)

SECTION 3 — RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS (RA)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Symmetric small joints (MCP, PIP, wrists)
- Morning stiffness >1 hour
- Warm, swollen joints
- Chronic, progressive pattern

Q1. Which joints hurt?
A:
- "Both hands and wrists."

Q2. Morning stiffness?
A:
- "Lasts a couple of hours."

Q3. Swelling?
A:
- "Yes, knuckles swollen."

Q4. Fatigue?
A:
- "Very tired."

RED FLAGS:
- Rapidly worsening hand swelling
- Difficulty making fist
- Systemic symptoms + joint pain (consider other diagnoses)

=========================================================
SECTION 4 — OSTEOARTHRITIS (OA)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Pain worse with activity
- Stiffness lasting <30 minutes
- Knees, hips, spine, hands (DIP, PIP)
- Bony enlargements (Heberden/Bouchard nodes)

Q1. Where is the pain?
A:
- "Knees and hips."

Q2. Worse with movement?
A:
- "Hurts when walking."

Q3. Morning stiffness?
A:
- "Only a few minutes."

Q4. Swelling?
A:
- "Some swelling after long day."

RED FLAGS:
- Knee swelling + fever (not OA)
- Night pain with weight loss (consider malignancy)

=========================================================
SECTION 5 — GOUT
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Sudden overnight onset
- Extremely painful, red, hot joint
- Big toe (1st MTP) common
- Triggers: alcohol, red meat, dehydration

Q1. Onset?
A:
- "Started suddenly at night."

Q2. Which joint?
A:
- "Big toe swollen and throbbing."

Q3. Appearance?
A:
- "Red, very tender, can’t tolerate touch."

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + red hot joint (possible septic arthritis)
- Severe unrelenting pain

=========================================================
SECTION 6 — PSEUDOGOUT
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Older adults
- Knee common
- Swelling + moderate redness
- Often triggered by illness/surgery

Q1. Which joint?
A:
- "My knee."

Q2. Onset?
A:
- "Started suddenly."

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + knee swelling + redness

=========================================================
SECTION 7 — LUPUS (SLE)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Young women more common
- Joint pain without destruction
- Photosensitive rash
- Malar butterfly rash
- Hair loss
- Mouth ulcers
- Fatigue
- Raynaud’s
- Chest pain (pleuritic)
- Kidney symptoms

Q1. Rashes?
A:
- "Rash on cheeks, worse in sun."

Q2. Hair loss?
A:
- "Hair falling out."

Q3. Mouth sores?
A:
- "Yes, painful sores."

Q4. Chest pain?
A:
- "Sharp pain when taking deep breath."

Q5. Urine?
A:
- "Foamy urine."

RED FLAGS:
- Chest pain + shortness of breath
- Kidney symptoms (edema, frothy urine)
- Fever + rash + joint pain

=========================================================
SECTION 8 — SJÖGREN’S SYNDROME
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Dry eyes ("gritty feeling")
- Dry mouth
- Parotid swelling
- Dental issues
- Fatigue
- Often overlaps with RA or lupus

Q1. Eye symptoms?
A:
- "Feels like sand in eyes."

Q2. Dry mouth?
A:
- "Hard to swallow crackers."

RED FLAGS:
- Severe dental decay
- Persistent parotid swelling

=========================================================
SECTION 9 — POLYMYALGIA RHEUMATICA (PMR)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Age >50
- Shoulder and hip girdle pain
- Morning stiffness >1 hour
- Difficulty rising from chair or lifting arms
- Often associated with giant cell arteritis (GCA)

Q1. Where is pain?
A:
- "Shoulders and hips."

Q2. Morning stiffness?
A:
- "Very stiff in morning, lasts hours."

Q3. Difficulty getting out of bed/chair?
A:
- "Yes, very hard to start moving."

RED FLAGS:
- Headache + jaw pain (GCA risk)
- Vision changes

=========================================================
SECTION 10 — ANKYLOSING SPONDYLITIS / AXIAL SPA
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Young men often
- Chronic back pain >3 months
- Morning stiffness >30–60 minutes
- Better with exercise, worse with rest
- Night back pain
- Heel/plantar pain
- Uveitis history

Q1. Back pain timeline?
A:
- "Years of back pain."

Q2. Worse at rest?
A:
- "Yes, stiff in mornings."

Q3. Better with exercise?
A:
- "Yes, moving helps."

Q4. Eye issues?
A:
- "Had painful red eye episodes."

RED FLAGS:
- New severe back pain + fever
- Neurologic deficits

=========================================================
SECTION 11 — PSORIATIC ARTHRITIS (PsA)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- History of psoriasis
- Nail pitting
- Dactylitis (sausage digits)
- Asymmetric oligoarthritis
- Back involvement possible

Q1. Skin history?
A:
- "Psoriasis for years."

Q2. Nail symptoms?
A:
- "Nail pitting."

Q3. Swollen fingers/toes?
A:
- "Whole finger looks swollen."

RED FLAGS:
- Severe joint destruction
- Sudden vision changes (uveitis risk)

=========================================================
SECTION 12 — REACTIVE ARTHRITIS
=========================================================

TRIAD:
- Arthritis
- Conjunctivitis
- Urethritis

Q1. Recent infection?
A:
- "Had stomach bug."
- "Had STI symptoms."

Q2. Eye symptoms?
A:
- "Red, painful eyes."

RED FLAGS:
- Severe eye pain
- Scrotal/testicular pain + joint pain (consider other causes)

=========================================================
SECTION 13 — VASCULITIS (HIGH-LEVEL PATTERN)
=========================================================

GENERAL CLUES:
- Purpuric rash
- Abdominal pain
- Neuropathy
- Kidney issues
- Fever, weight loss

HSP / IgA:
- Rash on legs
- Abdominal pain
- Joint pain

GCA:
- Headache, jaw claudication
- Vision symptoms

ANCA-associated:
- Sinusitis
- Cough with blood
- Kidney issues

RED FLAGS:
- Vision changes (GCA)
- Coughing blood
- Dark urine + swelling

SECTION 15 — SCLERODERMA (SYSTEMIC SCLEROSIS)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Tight skin
- Difficulty swallowing
- Heartburn
- Raynaud’s
- Puffy fingers
- Shortness of breath

Q1. Skin changes?
A:
- "Skin feels tight."

Q2. Heartburn?
A:
- "Bad reflux."

RED FLAGS:
- SOB + scleroderma features (pulmonary hypertension)
- Severe swallowing issues

=========================================================
SECTION 16 — MYOSITIS (DERMATOMYOSITIS / POLYMYOSITIS)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Proximal muscle weakness (difficulty lifting arms, climbing stairs)
- Rash (heliotrope, Gottron papules — high-level)
- Fatigue

Q1. Weakness?
A:
- "Hard to climb stairs."

Q2. Rash?
A:
- "Purple rash around eyes."

RED FLAGS:
- Difficulty swallowing
- Trouble breathing

=========================================================
SECTION 17 — FIBROMYALGIA (RHEUM-ADJACENT)
=========================================================

CLUES:
- Widespread pain
- Fatigue
- Poor sleep
- Cognitive fog

Q1. Does pain occur in multiple regions?
A:
- "Neck, shoulders, hips, back."

RED FLAGS:
- Night sweats + weight loss (not fibromyalgia)
- Focal neurologic deficits

=========================================================
SECTION 18 — RHEUMATOLOGY GLOBAL RED FLAGS (CONSOLIDATED)
=========================================================
- Hot, red, extremely painful joint (possible septic arthritis)
- Joint pain + fever
- Sudden swollen joint after injury (hemarthrosis)
- Back pain + bowel/bladder changes
- Rash + fever + joint pain
- Vision changes + headache (GCA)
- Unintentional weight loss + night sweats
- Severe muscle weakness (myositis)
- Chest pain + pleurisy symptoms (SLE)
