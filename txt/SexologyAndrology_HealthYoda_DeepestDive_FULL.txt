SECTION 1 — ERECTILE DYSFUNCTION (ED)
============================================================

CLUES:
- Difficulty achieving or maintaining erection
- Sudden vs gradual onset
- Morning erections present?
- Situational vs universal
- Pain or curve?

Q1. Problem getting or keeping erection?
A:
- “I can’t keep it hard.”

Q2. Morning erections?
A:
- “Still have some.” → psychogenic
- “None anymore.” → organic

COMMON CAUSES:
- Vascular disease
- Diabetes
- Hypertension
- Medications (SSRIs, beta-blockers)
- Low testosterone

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden complete ED after trauma  
- ED + penile pain + curve

SECTION 2 — LOW LIBIDO
============================================================

CLUES:
- Decreased sexual desire
- Fatigue
- Depression
- Partner-related stress
- Hormonal symptoms

Endocrine associations:

- Hypothyroid
- Hyperprolactinemia

Q1. Interest level?
A:
- “No desire lately.”

RED FLAGS:
- Abrupt major libido loss  
- Libido loss + testicular pain

SECTION 3 — EJACULATORY DISORDERS
============================================================

Premature Ejaculation:
- < 2 min consistently
- Lack of control

Delayed Ejaculation:
- Difficulty finishing
- Medication side effects

Retrograde Ejaculation:
- “Dry orgasm”
- Diabetes
- Alpha blockers

Q:
- “Does ejaculation happen too fast or not at all?”

A:
- “Happens too early.”
- “Takes forever, sometimes not at all.”
- “Very little comes out.”

============================================================

CLUES:
- Curvature developing slowly
- Pain with erection
- Difficulty with penetration
- Shortening

SECTION 5 — PENILE DISCHARGE & INFECTIONS
============================================================

CLUES:
- Discharge
- Burning urination
- Itching
- Swelling
- Ulcers/lesions

STIs to consider:
- Gonorrhea
- Chlamydia
- Herpes
- Syphilis
- Trichomonas

Q:
- “Color of discharge?”
A:
- “Yellow/green/white.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + swelling of penis  
- Painful ulcers  
- Scrotal swelling + fever  

============================================================
SECTION 6 — TESTICULAR PAIN DISORDERS
============================================================

TESTICULAR TORSION:
- Sudden pain
- Nausea
- Swelling
- High-riding testicle

EPIDIDYMITIS:
- Pain/swelling
- Gradual onset
- STI or UTI

VARICOCELE:
- “Bag of worms”
- Dull ache

HYDROCELE:
- Heavy sensation

Q:
- “Sudden or gradual?”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden severe pain  
- Pain + fever  
- Trauma + severe swelling

SECTION 8 — ENDOCRINE CAUSES
============================================================

- Klinefelter syndrome

SECONDARY:

Associated features:

- Breast enlargement

RED FLAGS:

- Gynecomastia  
- Severe fatigue

============================================================

- Penile fracture sound (“popping”)

- Painful ulcers  
- Inability to urinate  
- Testicular atrophy

SECTION 14 — TRIAGE LOGIC FOR HEALTHYODA
============================================================

ASK:
1. Erection issues?
2. Libido changes?
3. Pain anywhere?
4. Discharge?
5. Timing of pain?
6. Testicular position?
7. Trauma?
8. Fever?
9. Urinary symptoms?
10. Low mood/anxiety?
11. Medication changes?

ROUTE:
- Sudden testicular pain → torsion  
- ED + low morning erections → organic ED  
- ED + morning erections intact → psychological  
- Discharge + burning → STI  
- Curvature + pain → Peyronie’s  
- Libido loss + fatigue → endocrine  
- Dry orgasm → retrograde ejaculation
