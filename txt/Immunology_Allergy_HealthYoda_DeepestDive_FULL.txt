SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL ALLERGY/IMMUNOLOGY HPI STRUCTURE
=========================================================

ONSET:
- Sudden (minutes to hours)
- Subacute (hours to days)
- Chronic/recurrent (weeks to months)

TRIGGERS:
- Foods
- Medications
- Insect stings
- Environmental (pollen, pets, dust, mold)
- Latex
- Exercise
- Cold or heat

SYMPTOMS:
- Itching
- Hives
- Swelling
- Sneezing, runny nose, nasal congestion
- Itchy/watery eyes
- Cough/wheeze/shortness of breath
- GI symptoms (nausea, vomiting, pain, diarrhea)
- Dizziness, faintness

RISK FACTORS:
- Prior allergic reactions
- Asthma history
- Atopic history (eczema, allergic rhinitis)
- Family history of allergy
- Immunosuppressive meds

A: "Each spot lasts less than a day and moves around."

A: "Lips slightly swollen."

=========================================================

Q1. What is swollen?

Q2. Onset?
A: "Sudden swelling."

Q3. Associated with?
A: "New blood pressure medicine (ACE inhibitor)." / "Food exposure."

Q4. Breathing/swallowing?
A: "Hard to swallow." / "No trouble swallowing."

ANGIOEDEMA RED FLAGS:

- Trouble breathing or swallowing
- Change in voice/stridor

SECTION 5 — ANAPHYLAXIS PATTERNS
=========================================================

Typical combination:
- Skin/mucosa (hives, flushing, swelling)
- Respiratory (wheeze, throat tightness)
- GI (vomiting, cramps)
- Cardiovascular (dizziness, low BP)

Q1. What triggered it?
A: "Peanut." / "Bee sting." / "Medication."

Q2. Time from exposure to symptoms?
A: "Within 10–20 minutes."

Q3. Skin symptoms?
A: "Diffuse hives."

Q4. Breathing?
A: "Wheezing." / "Throat feels tight."

Q5. Circulation?
A: "Feeling faint."

ANAPHYLAXIS RED FLAGS (CRITICAL):
- Any respiratory compromise
- Any cardiovascular compromise
- Rapid symptom progression
- History of prior anaphylaxis

Q1. Cough or wheeze?

- Not responding to inhaler
- Blue lips or nail beds

SECTION 7 — ATOPIC DERMATITIS (ECZEMA)
=========================================================

Q1. Rash description?
A: "Dry, itchy, scaly patches."

Q2. Locations?
A: "Flexural creases (elbows, knees)." / "Hands."

Q3. Chronic or intermittent?
A: "Since childhood, flares in winter."

Q4. Triggers?
A: "Soaps, weather, stress."

ECZEMA RED FLAGS (LOW-ACUITY BUT NOTE):
- Weeping, crusted lesions (possible infection)
- Extensive body surface involvement

=========================================================

Q1. What medication and when started?
A: "Amoxicillin started 5 days ago."

Q2. Symptoms?
A:
- "Itchy rash."
- "Hives."
- "Facial swelling."

Q3. Timing vs dose?
A: "Rash started after second dose."

Q4. Respiratory or GI symptoms?
A: "Throat itching." / "Vomiting."

DRUG ALLERGY RED FLAGS:
- Hives + breathing difficulty
- Facial/tongue swelling
- Blistering rash, mucosal involvement (SJS/TEN pattern)

SECTION 15 — AUTOIMMUNE-TYPE COMPLAINTS (HIGH LEVEL)
=========================================================

Q1. Joint pain or swelling?
A: "Morning stiffness in hands."

Q2. Rashes?
A: "Butterfly-like rash on face." / "Sun-sensitive rash."

Q3. Mouth ulcers?
A: "Recurrent mouth sores."

AUTOIMMUNE RED FLAGS (for escalation):
- Joint pain + prolonged morning stiffness
- Rash + systemic symptoms (fever, weight loss)
- Hematuria or frothy urine with systemic complaints

=========================================================
SECTION 16 — VACCINATION REACTIONS
=========================================================

COMMON:
- Sore arm
- Low-grade fever
- Mild fatigue

CONCERNING:
- Immediate hives
- Lip/tongue swelling
- Trouble breathing
- High fever and ill appearance

=========================================================
IMMUNOLOGY / ALLERGY GLOBAL RED FLAGS
=========================================================
- Any trouble breathing
- Wheezing or stridor
- Lip, tongue, or throat swelling
- Hives with dizziness/faintness
- Recurrent severe infections
- Blistering skin + mucosal involvement after new drug
- Persistent high fever with rash and systemic symptoms
