SECTION 1 — URETHRAL DISCHARGE SYNDROMES
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Urethral discharge
- Burning urination
- Penile/vaginal itching
- Odor changes

Likely:
- Chlamydia
- Gonorrhea
- Trichomonas

Q:
- “Color of discharge?”
A:
- “Yellow/green/white.”
- “Thin/clear.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + discharge + testicular pain

=====================================================================
SECTION 2 — GENITAL ULCER SYNDROMES
=====================================================================

Causes:
- Herpes (HSV)
- Syphilis
- Chancroid
- Behçet mimicker

CLUES:
- Painful vs painless ulcers
- Recurrent vs first time
- Multiple or single

Painful ulcers:
→ HSV, chancroid

Painless ulcers:
→ syphilis

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + ulcers
- Rapid spreading ulcers
- Severe pain preventing urination

=====================================================================
SECTION 3 — CERVICITIS PATTERNS
=====================================================================

CLUES (female):
- Vaginal discharge
- Bleeding after sex
- Pelvic pain
- Burning urination

Likely:
- Chlamydia
- Gonorrhea
- Trichomonas
- Mycoplasma genitalium

RED FLAGS:
- Pain + fever
- Bleeding + fever

=====================================================================
SECTION 4 — PELVIC INFLAMMATORY DISEASE (PID)
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Pelvic pain
- Fever
- Vaginal discharge
- Pain with sex
- Pain with movement

High-risk:
- Untreated chlamydia/gonorrhea

Q1:
- “Lower abdominal pain?”

A:
- “Yes, sharp.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + pelvic pain
- Severe pain + fainting

=====================================================================
SECTION 5 — EPIDIDYMO-ORCHITIS
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Testicular pain
- Swelling
- Dysuria
- Fever

Common STI causes:
- Chlamydia
- Gonorrhea

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden severe pain (torsion risk)
- High fever

SECTION 14 — TRIAGE LOGIC FOR HEALTHYODA
=====================================================================

ASK:
1. Discharge?
2. Ulcers?
3. Pain? where?
4. Fever?
5. Bleeding after sex?
6. Testicular pain?
7. Pain with sex?
8. Rectal pain?
9. Systemic symptoms?
10. HIV exposure risk?

ROUTE:
- Fever + pelvic pain → PID
- Fever + ulcers → severe HSV
- Penile discharge + burning → GC/CT
- Testicular pain + dysuria → epididymitis
- Mouth ulcers + rash → secondary syphilis
- Fever + rash → acute HIV
