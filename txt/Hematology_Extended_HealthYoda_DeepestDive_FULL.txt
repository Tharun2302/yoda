SECTION 1 — ANEMIA TYPES (MICRO, NORMO, MACRO)
=========================================================

Q1. What are your main symptoms?
A:
- "Fatigue."
- "Short of breath on exertion."
- "Dizzy when standing."
- "Cold hands and feet."

MICROCYTIC (IRON DEFICIENCY, THALASSEMIA):
A:
- "Craving ice" (pica)
- "Heavy periods"
- "Chronic blood loss symptoms"

NORMOCYTIC (CHRONIC DISEASE, RENAL):
A:
- "Fatigue without other symptoms"
- "History of kidney problems"

MACROCYTIC (B12/FOLATE):
A:
- "Numb feet"
- "Memory trouble"
- "Burning tongue"

RED FLAGS:
- Chest pain with anemia
- Syncope
- Dark urine + jaundice (hemolysis)

SECTION 10 — LEUKEMIA (ACUTE VS CHRONIC)
=========================================================

ACUTE:
- Fever
- Night sweats
- Bleeding/bruising
- Bone pain
- Fatigue

CHRONIC:
- Fatigue
- Fullness under left ribs (splenomegaly)
- Early satiety

RED FLAGS:
- Persistent fever
- Bruising
- Recurrent infections

=========================================================
SECTION 11 — LYMPHOMA
=========================================================

HODGKIN:
- Neck/armpit lymph nodes
- Night sweats
- Fever
- Weight loss

NON-HODGKIN:
- Enlarging painless nodes
- Fatigue
- Chest/abdominal fullness

RED FLAGS:
- Lump growing >4 weeks
- Night sweats + weight loss
- Itching + lymph node swelling

SECTION 17 — MULTIPLE MYELOMA
=========================================================

CRAB (high-level symptom cues):
- Calcium high → "Thirst, confusion"
- Renal failure → "Fatigue, swelling"
- Anemia → "Tired"
- Bone pain → "Back pain, worse at night"

RED FLAGS:
- Bone pain + weight loss
- Recurrent infections
- New fractures without trauma

=========================================================
SECTION 18 — TRANSFUSION REACTIONS
=========================================================

MILD:
- Itching
- Rash

MODERATE:
- Fever
- Chills

SEVERE:
- Back pain
- Hypotension
- Dark urine

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + back pain + dark urine during transfusion

RED FLAGS:

=========================================================
SECTION 20 — GLOBAL HEMATOLOGY RED FLAGS
=========================================================
- Fatigue + fever + bruising (possible leukemia)
- Night sweats + weight loss + lymph nodes
- Leg swelling + SOB (possible PE)
- Chest pain + severe anemia
- Dark urine + jaundice
- Bone pain at night
- Unexplained recurrent infections
- Black or bloody stools
- Sudden severe headache on anticoagulation
