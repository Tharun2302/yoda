SECTION 1 — QUADRANT-BASED PAIN DISCRIMINATION
===========================================================

RUQ PAIN:
- Gallstones, cholecystitis
- Hepatitis
- Liver congestion
- Pulmonary (RLL pneumonia)

Q:
- “Pain gets worse after fatty foods?”
A:
- “Yes, after meals.”

Epigastric pain:
- Gastritis, PUD
- Pancreatitis
- MI equivalent

RLQ PAIN:
- Appendicitis
- Ovarian torsion
- Ectopic pregnancy
- Kidney stones
- Crohn’s flare

LLQ PAIN:
- Diverticulitis
- Ovarian cyst
- Constipation

RED FLAGS:
- Rebound tenderness
- Severe RUQ pain + fever (cholecystitis)
- RLQ pain + fever (appendicitis)
- LLQ pain + fever (diverticulitis)

SECTION 2 — PERITONITIS VS NON-PERITONITIS
===========================================================

PERITONITIS CLUES:
- Severe pain with movement
- Guarding
- Rigidity (history-level)
- Pain worse with bumps in car

Q:
- “Does moving make it worse?”
A:
- “Yes, even bumps hurt.”

NON-PERITONITIS:
- Crampy pain
- Spasms
- Waves of pain (colicky)

===========================================================
SECTION 3 — SURGICAL ABDOMINAL EMERGENCIES
===========================================================

APPENDICITIS:
- RLQ pain
- Loss of appetite
- Nausea
- Fever

Q:
- “Starts around belly button then moves?”
A:
- “Yes.”

PERFORATION:
- Sudden severe pain
- Shoulder tip pain (diaphragm irritation)
- Rigidity

BOWEL ISCHEMIA:
- Pain out of proportion
- Bloody stools

ECTOPIC PREGNANCY:
- Early pregnancy + pain + bleeding
- Shoulder pain

Torsion (ovarian/testicular):
- Sudden severe lower abdominal/groin pain
- Nausea/vomiting

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden severe abdominal pain  
- Pain + fainting  
- Severe pain + vaginal bleeding  
- Pain out of proportion  

===========================================================
SECTION 4 — GI OBSTRUCTION SPECTRUM
===========================================================

SMALL BOWEL OBSTRUCTION:
- Cramping
- Vomiting
- Abdominal distension
- No gas/stool

LARGE BOWEL OBSTRUCTION:
- Constipation
- Progressive distension
- Pain milder at first

Q:
- “Passing gas?”
A:
- “No.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe distension + no stool  
- Persistent vomiting  

===========================================================
SECTION 5 — VASCULAR ABDOMINAL EMERGENCIES
===========================================================

AAA RUPTURE:
- Sudden back/abdominal pain
- Dizziness/fainting
- Pulsating sensation

AORTIC DISSECTION:
- Tearing chest/back/abdominal pain

MESENTERIC ISCHEMIA:
- Pain out of proportion
- Vomiting
- Blood in stool

RED FLAGS:
- Pain + faintness  
- Pain out of proportion  
- Older + smoker + sudden pain  

===========================================================
SECTION 6 — BILIARY + PANCREATIC DISORDERS
===========================================================

CHOLECYSTITIS:
- RUQ pain radiating to shoulder/back
- Fever
- Pain after fatty meals

PANCREATITIS:
- Severe epigastric pain radiating to back
- Worse after food
- Nausea/vomiting
- History alcohol/gallstones

Q:
- “Does pain go to the back?”
A:
- “Yes.”

RED FLAGS:
- RUQ pain + fever  
- Severe epigastric pain + vomiting  
- Pain radiating to back  

===========================================================
SECTION 7 — GI BLEEDING PRESENTATIONS
===========================================================

UPPER GI BLEED:
- Vomiting blood
- Black stools
- Dizziness

LOWER GI BLEED:
- Bright blood
- Cramping
- Diarrhea

Q:
- “What color is stool?”
A:
- “Black / bright red.”

RED FLAGS:
- Vomiting blood  
- Black stools + dizziness  

===========================================================
SECTION 8 — INFECTIOUS GI
===========================================================

GASTROENTERITIS:
- Diarrhea
- Nausea/vomiting
- Cramping

C. DIFF:
- Profuse diarrhea
- Recent antibiotics
- Abdominal tenderness

DIVERTICULITIS:
- LLQ pain
- Fever
- Constipation/diarrhea

Q:
- “Any recent antibiotics?”
A:
- “Yes recently.”

SECTION 9 — RENAL/URINARY ABDOMINAL PAIN
===========================================================

KIDNEY STONES:
- Severe flank pain
- Radiates to groin
- Nausea/vomiting
- Blood in urine

UTI/PYELONEPHRITIS:
- Fever
- Back pain
- Burning urination

Q:
- “Burning or frequency?”
A:
- “Yes.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + flank pain  
- Severe flank pain

SECTION 10 — REPRODUCTIVE CAUSES
===========================================================

OVARIAN TORSION:
- Sudden pain
- Nausea/vomiting

ECTOPIC PREGNANCY:
- Early pregnancy
- Pain + bleeding
- Shoulder pain

PELVIC INFLAMMATORY DISEASE (PID):
- Pelvic pain
- Fever

TESTICULAR TORSION:
- Sudden groin pain
- Nausea

SECTION 11 — METABOLIC / ENDOCRINE ABDOMINAL PAIN
===========================================================

DKA:
- Abdominal pain + vomiting
- Thirst
- Deep breathing

ADRENAL CRISIS:
- Abdominal pain
- Vomiting
- Weakness
- Dizziness

RED FLAGS:
- Abdominal pain + fainting  
- Vomiting + deep breathing

SECTION 12 — PEDIATRIC ABDOMINAL PAIN
===========================================================

INTUSSUSCEPTION:
- Severe crampy pain
- Episodes of crying
- Red currant jelly stools

APPENDICITIS:
- RLQ pain
- Fever

CONSTIPATION:
- Chronic
- Decreased appetite

MECKEL’S:
- Painless bleeding

RED FLAGS:
- Severe crying/colic episodes  
- Bloody stools

SECTION 13 — CHRONIC ABDOMINAL PAIN PATTERNS
===========================================================

IBS:
- Cramping relieved by stool
- Alternating diarrhea/constipation

IBD:
- Bloody stools
- Weight loss
- Fever

CELIAC:
- Bloating
- Diarrhea
- Weight loss

ULCER DISEASE:
- Epigastric pain, worse at night

SECTION 14 — GLOBAL ABDOMINAL PAIN RED FLAGS
===========================================================
- Sudden severe abdominal pain  
- Pain + fainting  
- Pain out of proportion  
- Vomiting blood  
- Black stools  
- Severe distension  
- Fever + abdominal pain  
- Pain in pregnancy  
- Bloody stool

SECTION 15 — ABDOMINAL PAIN TRIAGE LOGIC FOR HEALTHYODA
===========================================================

ASK:
1. Location of pain?
2. Onset?
3. Nausea/vomiting?
4. Diarrhea/constipation?
5. Blood in stool?
6. Passing gas?
7. Fever?
8. Urinary symptoms?
9. Pregnancy?
10. Chest pain/SOB?
11. Radiation (back, shoulder, groin)?

ROUTE:
- RLQ pain + fever → appendicitis  
- RUQ pain + fever → cholecystitis  
- Severe epigastric pain → pancreatitis  
- Severe flank pain → kidney stone  
- Sudden pain + fainting → AAA/ectopic  
- Pain + no gas/stool → obstruction  
- Pain out of proportion → ischemia  
- Pain + black stools → GI bleed  
- Pain in pregnancy → ectopic/PID/UTI
