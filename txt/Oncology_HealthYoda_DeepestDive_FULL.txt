SECTION 1 — CONSTITUTIONAL SYMPTOMS (B SYMPTOMS)
=========================================================

WEIGHT LOSS
-----------
Q1. How much weight have you lost?
A: "About 15 pounds."

Q2. Over what time?
A: "2 months."

Q3. Intentional?
A: "No I’m eating normally."

RED FLAGS:
- >10% body weight over 6 months
- Weight loss + night sweats
- Weight loss + lymph nodes

NIGHT SWEATS
------------
Q1. How severe?
A: "Sheets soaked."

Q2. Fever?
A: "Low fevers."

RED FLAGS:
- Drenching sweats
- Sweats + weight loss + nodes (lymphoma pattern)

FATIGUE
-------
Q1. Severity?
A: "Severe fatigue."

Q2. Impacting daily life?
A: "Can’t perform usual tasks."

SECTION 2 — LYMPH NODE ENLARGEMENT
=========================================================

Q1. Where?
A: "Neck." / "Armpit." / "Groin."

Q2. Painful or painless?
A: "Painless lump."

Q3. Duration?
A: "Present for weeks."

Q4. Growing?
A: "Getting bigger."

RED FLAGS:
- Painless enlarging nodes
- Nodes in multiple regions
- Night sweats + weight loss
- Hard, fixed masses

SECTION 9 — SKIN SYMPTOMS (MELANOMA/OTHER)
=========================================================

ABCDE:
- Asymmetry
- Border irregular
- Color variation
- Diameter >6mm
- Evolution

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid changes
- Bleeding mole
- Irregular borders

SECTION 10 — PARANEOPLASTIC SYNDROMES
=========================================================

Possible symptoms:
- Muscle weakness
- Neuropathy
- Hyponatremia symptoms (confusion)
- Hypercalcemia (constipation, kidney stones)

SECTION 11 — CANCER TREATMENT COMPLICATIONS
=========================================================

CHEMOTHERAPY:
- Fever (neutropenia)
- Severe nausea
- Mouth sores
- Diarrhea

IMMUNOTHERAPY:
- Rash
- Diarrhea
- Cough/SOB (pneumonitis)

SURGERY:
- Pain
- Infection signs

ONCOLOGY GLOBAL RED FLAGS
=========================================================
- Unintentional weight loss >10%
- Drenching night sweats
- Painless enlarged lymph nodes
- Persistent cough >6 weeks
- Hemoptysis
- Black stools or bloody stools
- Painless testicular lump
- Severe persistent headaches
- Focal neurologic deficits
- Fever in chemo patient
- Shortness of breath + chest pain
- Breast lump with skin/nipple changes
