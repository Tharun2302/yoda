SECTION 2 — BLURRY VISION
=========================================================

Q1. When did it start?
A: "Suddenly this morning." / "Gradually over months."

Q2. One or both eyes?
A: "Left eye." / "Both eyes."

Q3. Constant or intermittent?
A: "Constant blur." / "Intermittent."

Q4. Painful or painless?
A: "Painless." / "Mild pain behind eye."

Q5. Redness or discharge?
A: "No redness." / "Watery eye."

BLURRY VISION RED FLAGS:
- Sudden unilateral vision loss
- Blurry vision + headache (glaucoma)
- Blurry vision + flashes/floaters
- Diabetic hyperglycemia

=========================================================
SECTION 3 — SUDDEN VISION LOSS
=========================================================

Q1. Describe the vision loss.
A: "Fog over eye." / "Everything went black."

Q2. One or both eyes?
A: "Right eye only."

Q3. Flashes or floaters?
A: "Saw flashing lights."

Q4. Headache?
A: "Severe headache."

SUDDEN VISION LOSS RED FLAGS:
- "Curtain over vision" (retinal detachment)
- Painless sudden loss (CRAO)
- Painful loss + red eye (glaucoma)
- Vision loss + jaw pain (GCA >50)
- Vision loss after trauma

SECTION 4 — DOUBLE VISION
=========================================================

Q1. Does it go away with one eye closed?
A: "Yes." (binocular)

Q2. Onset?
A: "Started today."

Q3. Associated symptoms?
A: Headache, weakness, eyelid droop.

DIPLOPIA RED FLAGS:
- Painful diplopia
- Diplopia + weakness (stroke)
- Diplopia after trauma
