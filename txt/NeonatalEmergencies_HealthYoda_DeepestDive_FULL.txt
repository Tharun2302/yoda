SECTION 1 — NEONATAL SEPSIS
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Poor feeding
- Lethargy
- Weak cry
- Low temperature OR fever
- Irritability
- Rapid breathing

Q:
- “How is feeding?”
A:
- “Barely taking anything.”

RED FLAGS:
- Feeding refusal
- Hypothermia
- Grunting
- Lethargy

=====================================================================
SECTION 2 — FEVER vs LOW TEMP
=====================================================================

FEVER:
>38.0°C (100.4°F)

LOW TEMP:
<36°C (96.8°F)

Q:
- “Temp reading?”

A:
- “Cold to touch, 35.7°C”

Hypothermia may indicate sepsis.

RED FLAGS:
- Any fever in newborn
- Any low temp in newborn

=====================================================================
SECTION 3 — NEONATAL RESPIRATORY DISTRESS
=====================================================================

SIGNS:
- Retractions
- Nasal flaring
- Grunting
- Rapid RR
- Poor feeding
- Cyanosis

Consider:
- TTN
- RDS
- Pneumonia
- Pneumothorax
- Congenital heart disease

RED FLAGS:
- Blue lips
- Pauses in breathing

=====================================================================
SECTION 4 — NEONATAL JAUNDICE EMERGENCIES
=====================================================================

RED FLAGS:
- Jaundice within first 24 hrs
- Rapid rise
- Poor feeding
- Lethargy
- Arching
- High-pitched cry

Consider:
- Hemolysis
- Sepsis
- Breastfeeding failure jaundice

=====================================================================
SECTION 5 — FEEDING FAILURE EMERGENCIES
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Poor latch
- Weak suck
- Falling asleep during feeds
- Vomiting
- No stool/urine

RED FLAGS:
- No urine for >12 hrs
- No stool for 48 hrs
- Lethargy + poor feeding

=====================================================================
SECTION 6 — HYPOGLYCEMIA
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Jitteriness
- Lethargy
- Poor feeding
- Hypothermia

RED FLAGS:
- Jittery + poor feeding

=====================================================================
SECTION 7 — NEONATAL DEHYDRATION
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Few wet diapers
- Dry mouth
- Poor feeding
- Weight loss >10%

RED FLAGS:
- Sunken fontanelle
- No wet diaper >12 hrs

=====================================================================
SECTION 8 — CARDIAC EMERGENCIES
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Cyanosis
- Poor feeding
- Fast breathing
- Sweating

Consider:
- Congenital heart disease
- Ductal-dependent lesions

RED FLAGS:
- Cyanosis unresponsive to oxygen

=====================================================================
SECTION 9 — GI EMERGENCIES
=====================================================================

CAUSES:
- Pyloric stenosis
- Malrotation
- NEC
- Intestinal obstruction

CLUES:
- Bilious vomiting
- Bloody stools
- Abdominal distension

RED FLAGS:
- Bilious vomit
- Bloody stool
- Severe abdominal distention

=====================================================================
SECTION 10 — SEIZURES in NEONATES
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Lip smacking
- Eye deviation
- Tremors not suppressed

RED FLAGS:
- ANY seizure activity

=====================================================================
SECTION 11 — BIRTH TRAUMA COMPLICATIONS
=====================================================================

CLUES:
- Irritability
- Poor feeding
- Asymmetry
- Reduced movement
- Bruising/swelling

=====================================================================
SECTION 12 — CONGENITAL ANOMALIES
=====================================================================

EMERGENCY SIGNS:
- Cyanosis
- Inability to feed
- Vomiting
- Abnormal breathing noises

=====================================================================
SECTION 13 — GLOBAL NEONATAL EMERGENCY RED FLAGS
=====================================================================
- Fever ANY
- Hypothermia ANY
- Weak cry
- Poor feeding
- Lethargy
- Gasping / rapid breathing
- Blue lips
- Bilious vomiting
- Bloody stool
- No wet diapers
- Seizures

=====================================================================
SECTION 14 — HEALTHYODA TRIAGE LOGIC
=====================================================================

ASK:
1. Feeding?
2. Urine?
3. Stools?
4. Breathing?
5. Temp?
6. Color?
7. Cry strength?
8. Awake/responsive?

ROUTE:
- Fever OR hypothermia → sepsis pathway
- Poor feeding + low urine → dehydration
- Cyanosis + distress → cardiac/resp
- Bilious vomiting → GI obstruction
- Seizure → immediate escalation
