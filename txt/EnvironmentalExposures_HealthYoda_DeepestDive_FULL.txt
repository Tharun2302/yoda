SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL ENVIRONMENTAL EXPOSURE HPI STRUCTURE
=========================================================

TIMING:
- Immediate
- Hours later
- Chronic exposure over months/years

DURATION:
- Single event
- Repeated exposure
- Continuous exposure

SETTING:
- Workplace
- Home
- Outdoor environment
- Industrial facility
- Car/garage
- Recent travel

SYMPTOMS:
- Headache
- Nausea
- Breathing difficulty
- Chest tightness
- Dizziness
- Eye/nose/throat irritation
- Skin rash or irritation
- Neurologic changes
- GI symptoms
- Fatigue

EXPOSURE QUESTIONS:
- What were you doing?
- For how long?
- Protective equipment?
- Anyone else affected?
- Changes in environment (new cleaners, construction)?

=========================================================

Q1. What exposure?

Q2. Symptoms?
A:
- “Coughing”

RED FLAGS:

- Chest tightness

- Severe coughing fits

SECTION 3 — SMOKE INHALATION (FIRE/WILDFIRE)
=========================================================

Q1. Indoors or outdoors?
A: “Smoke inside house.”

Q2. Soot in mouth/nose?
A: “Black soot when coughing.”

Q3. Symptoms?
A:
- “Headache”
- “Chest tightness”
- “Coughing”

SMOKE RED FLAGS:
- Carbon monoxide symptoms (headache, nausea)
- Confusion
- Burns around nose/mouth
- Hoarseness
- Difficulty breathing

SECTION 4 — MOLD EXPOSURE
=========================================================

Q1. Visible mold?
A: “Black mold in bathroom.”

Q2. Symptoms?
A:
- “Chronic cough”
- “Worse in house”
- “Fatigue”
- “Nasal congestion”

RED FLAGS:
- Wheezing
- Fever + shortness of breath
- Immunocompromised patient

=========================================================

SYMPTOMS (SLUDGE):

- Urination
- Defecation
- GI upset
- Emesis

RED FLAGS:

- Seizures
- Confusion

=========================================================
SECTION 10 — RADIATION EXPOSURE
=========================================================

Low-dose symptoms:
- Fatigue
- Nausea

High-dose symptoms:
- Vomiting within 1–2 hours
- Skin redness
- Confusion

RED FLAGS:
- Vomiting within 1 hour of exposure
- Neurologic changes
- Bleeding/bruising

=========================================================
SECTION 11 — WATER CONTAMINATION
=========================================================

Possible contaminants:
- Bacteria
- Parasites
- Heavy metals

Symptoms:
- Diarrhea
- Abdominal pain
- Fatigue
- Metallic taste

RED FLAGS:
- Bloody diarrhea
- Severe dehydration
- Fever + abdominal pain

=========================================================
SECTION 12 — HEAT ILLNESS (HEAT EXHAUSTION/STROKE)
=========================================================

Q1. Activity?
A: “Working outside.”

Symptoms:
- Heavy sweating → exhaustion
- No sweating → stroke
- Confusion
- Throbbing headache

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Body temp >103°F
- No sweating
- Seizure

=========================================================
SECTION 13 — COLD EXPOSURE (HYPOTHERMIA/FROSTBITE)
=========================================================

Symptoms:
- Shivering → early
- Confusion
- Numb fingers/toes
- Pale/mottled skin

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Frostbite blistering
- Core temp <95°F

=========================================================
SECTION 14 — NOISE EXPOSURE
=========================================================

Q1. Duration?
A: “Concert last night.”

Symptoms:
- Ringing ears
- Temporary hearing loss

RED FLAGS:
- Persistent hearing loss
- Vertigo + hearing loss

SECTION 17 — HEAVY METAL EXPOSURES (NON-LEAD)
=========================================================

- Tremor

- Vomiting
- Abdominal pain
- Numbness/tingling

RED FLAGS:
- GI bleeding
- Severe weakness

=========================================================
SECTION 18 — GAS LEAKS / VENTILATION FAILURE
=========================================================

Symptoms:
- Headache
- Dizziness
- Nausea

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion
- Fainting
- Multiple people sick

=========================================================
ENVIRONMENTAL EXPOSURE GLOBAL RED FLAGS
=========================================================
- Breathing difficulty or wheezing
- Confusion, fainting, or altered mental status
- Vomiting + exposure to chemicals
- Cyanosis or blue lips
- Severe chest pain
- High fever with environmental exposure
- Children exposed to chemicals, CO, or heavy metals
- Multiple people symptomatic simultaneously
