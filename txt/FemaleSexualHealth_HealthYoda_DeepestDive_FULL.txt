SECTION 1 — FEMALE SEXUAL PAIN DISORDERS
=============================================================

Dyspareunia:
- Pain with penetration
- Superficial vs deep pain

Q1. Where is the pain felt?
A:
- “External, right at entry.”
- “Deep inside.”

If deep pain → consider:
- Endometriosis
- PID
- Ovarian cysts
- Uterine fibroids

If superficial:
- Vaginismus
- Vulvodynia
- Dryness

RED FLAGS:
- Pain with fever  
- Pain + bleeding  
- New severe pain

SECTION 2 — FEMALE SEXUAL AROUSAL DISORDER
=============================================================

CLUES:
- Difficulty becoming lubricated
- Difficulty becoming physically aroused
- Decreased genital sensitivity

Q1. Arousal difficulty?
A:
- “Hard to get turned on physically.”

ADDITIONAL CLUES:
- Medications
- Hormone loss
- Stress

SECTION 3 — FEMALE ORGASM DISORDER
=============================================================

CLUES:
- Delayed or absent orgasm
- Normal desire but difficulty climaxing
- Medication-induced

Q1. Difficulty reaching orgasm?
A:
- “Takes very long / doesn’t happen.”

MEDICATION CAUSES:
- SSRIs
- Antipsychotics

SECTION 7 — HORMONAL PATTERNS
=============================================================

Menopause-related:
- Hot flashes
- Dryness
- Low libido
- Mood swings

Perimenopause patterns:
- Irregular cycles
- Variable symptoms

Low Estrogen:
- Vaginal dryness
- Pain
- Low libido

Low Testosterone:
- Low desire
- Fatigue
- Loss of strength

SECTION 11 — STI-RELATED PRESENTATIONS
=============================================================

SYMPTOMS:
- Pelvic pain
- Fever
- Vaginal discharge
- Odor change
- Bleeding after sex
- Burning urination
- Ulcers/sores

Consider:
- Chlamydia
- Gonorrhea
- Trichomonas
- HSV
- Syphilis

RED FLAGS:
- Fever + pelvic pain  
- Severe abdominal/pelvic pain  
- Purulent discharge

SECTION 12 — OB/GYN RED FLAGS (CRITICAL)
=============================================================
- Severe pelvic pain  
- Pain with fever  
- Sudden bleeding  
- Pain + fainting  
- Ulcers  
- Vaginal discharge + fever  
- Severe lower abdominal pain  
- Positive pregnancy + pain  
- Pain after childbirth

SECTION 13 — TRIAGE LOGIC FOR HEALTHYODA
=============================================================

ASK:
1. Pain or no pain?
2. Where is pain located?
3. Is dryness present?
4. Is arousal difficult?
5. Is orgasm difficult?
6. Any trauma?
7. Any discharge?
8. Bleeding?
9. Fever?
10. Medications?
11. New stress or relationship issue?

ROUTE:
- Pain + fever → PID  
- Pain + deep pelvic pain → endometriosis  
- External burning → vulvodynia  
- Dryness + menopause signs → low estrogen  
- Difficulty orgasming + SSRI → med-induced  
- Pain with penetration → vaginismus  
- Desire loss + fatigue → hormonal
