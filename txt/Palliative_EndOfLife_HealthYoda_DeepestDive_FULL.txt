SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL PALLIATIVE HISTORY STRUCTURE
======================================================================

Q1. What symptoms are most troubling right now?
A:
- “Breathing is hard.”
- “My pain is bad today.”
- “Too weak to stand.”
- “No appetite.”

Q2. How long have these symptoms been getting worse?
A:
- “Past few weeks.”
- “Rapid worsening in 2–3 days.”

Q3. Any recent hospitalizations or major changes?
A:
- “Was hospitalized last month.”

Q4. Can you get out of bed?
A:
- “Mostly bedbound.”
- “Only for short times.”

Q5. Eating/drinking?
A:
- “Very little.”

Q6. What helps or worsens symptoms?
A:
- “Sitting up helps breathing.”
- “Pain worse when moving.”

GLOBAL END-OF-LIFE RED FLAGS:
- New severe dyspnea  
- Sudden change in mental status  
- Minimal intake + profound weakness  
- Rapid functional decline  
- Restlessness/agitation with confusion  
- Terminal secretions (“death rattle”)  
- Somnolence → unresponsiveness

SECTION 2 — PAIN IN END-OF-LIFE CONTEXT
======================================================================

TYPES:
- Nociceptive (bone, muscle)
A: “Deep aching pain.”
- Neuropathic
A: “Burning, shooting pain.”
- Visceral
A: “Cramping pressure in abdomen.”

Q1. Where is the pain?
A:
- “Back and ribs.”
- “Cancer site pain.”

Q2. Constant or comes and goes?
A:
- “Constant with sharp spikes.”

Q3. Does it wake you from sleep?
A:
- “Yes.”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden new severe pain  
- Pain + confusion (possibly undertreated or delirium)  
- Pain with new neurologic deficits

SECTION 3 — DYSPNEA / BREATHING DIFFICULTY
======================================================================

COMMON CAUSES:
- Cancer progression
- Fluid overload
- Weak respiratory muscles
- Anxiety overlay
- Terminal secretions

Q1. When is breathing worst?
A:
- “When lying flat.”
- “All the time, worse with movement.”

Q2. Cough?
A:
- “Dry cough.”
- “Wet rattling.”

Q3. Associated symptoms?
A:
- “Chest tightness.”
- “Anxiety.”

RED FLAGS:
- Rapid increase in work of breathing  
- Gasps or pauses  
- Cyanosis

SECTION 7 — NAUSEA & VOMITING
======================================================================

CAUSE CATEGORIES:
- Medication/opioid
- GI obstruction
- Metabolic disturbances
- Brain metastases
- Infection

Q1. How often do you feel nauseous?
A:
- “All day.”
- “Worse after meds.”

Q2. Vomiting?
A:
- “Occasional.”
- “Frequent.”

Q3. Type?
A:
- “Green/yellow.”
- “Undigested food.”

RED FLAGS:
- Coffee-ground vomit  
- Projectile vomiting  
- Inability to keep liquids

======================================================================

HYPERACTIVE:
- Agitation
- Hallucinations
- Confusion

HYPOACTIVE:
- Sleepy
- Hard to wake
- Less responsive

Q1. Mental clarity?
A:
- “Feels confused.”

Q2. Visual hallucinations?
A:
- “Seeing things.”

Q3. Awake level?
A:
- “Sleeping most of time.”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden onset  
- Delirium + fever (infection)  
- Delirium + new pain

SECTION 17 — END-OF-LIFE TRAJECTORY CLUES
======================================================================

FINAL WEEKS:
- Profound fatigue  
- Limited intake  
- Increasing sleep  
- Weakness  

FINAL DAYS:
- Very little intake  
- Sleeping most of time  
- Cool extremities  
- Irregular breathing  

FINAL HOURS:
- Cheyne–Stokes breathing  
- Terminal secretions  
- Minimal responsiveness  
- Hands/feet mottled
