SECTION 1 — FEVER (AGE-SPECIFIC LOGIC)
=========================================================

Q1. Child’s age?
A: "6 months." / "2 years." / "8 years."

Q2. Highest temperature?
A: "102°F." / "104°F."

Q3. Duration?
A: "Started today." / "3 days."

Q4. Symptoms?
A: "Cough." / "Vomiting." / "Rash."

INFANT FEVER RED FLAGS (0–3 months):
- Fever ≥100.4°F in infant <3 months (emergent)
- Poor feeding
- Lethargy
- Weak cry
- Trouble breathing

CHILD FEVER RED FLAGS:
- Fever >105°F
- Stiff neck
- Purple rash
- Severe headache
- Breathing difficulty

=========================================================

A: "Drinking half usual milk."

Q2. Wet diapers?
A: "Only 2 today."

POOR FEEDING RED FLAGS:
- <3 wet diapers/day
- Refuses all feeds
- Lethargy
- Dry mouth, no tears

Q2. Diarrhea?
A: "Loose watery."

Q3. Able to keep fluids?
A: "Vomits even sips."

Q4. Urine output?
A: "Few wet diapers."

GI RED FLAGS:
- Bloody stools
- Bilious (green) vomiting
- Severe abdominal pain
- No urine output
- Lethargy

SECTION 4 — ABDOMINAL PAIN
=========================================================

Q1. Pain location?
A: "Around belly button." / "Right side."

Q2. Severity?
A: "Crying in pain."

Q3. Vomiting?
A: "Vomited once."

Q4. Walking?
A: "Doesn’t want to walk."

ABD PAIN RED FLAGS:
- RLQ pain + fever (appendicitis)
- Severe pain + vomiting
- Blood in stools
- Inability to walk
- Pain + testicular pain (torsion)

=========================================================
SECTION 17 — PEDIATRIC EMERGENCY RED FLAGS
=========================================================
- Fever <3 months
- Trouble breathing / retractions
- Blue lips
- Unresponsiveness
- Seizure >5 minutes
- Stiff neck
- Petechial/purpuric rash
- Not drinking + no urine
- Severe abdominal pain
- Testicular pain
- Bulging fontanelle
- High-pitched cry
- Head injury with vomiting
