=======================================================
SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL SHOCK RECOGNITION (SYMPTOMS ONLY)
=======================================================

CORE SYMPTOMS:
- Severe weakness
- Dizziness/lightheadedness
- Confusion
- Feeling faint
- Shortness of breath
- Cold/clammy or hot/flushed skin
- Rapid heart rate or very slow
- Low urine output (“not peeing much”)
- Sense of doom

Q1. How do you feel?
A:
- “Very weak and dizzy.”
- “Feel like I’m going to pass out.”

Q2. Breathing?
A:
- “Hard to breathe.”
- “Breathing fast.”

Q3. Skin temperature?
A:
- “Cold and sweaty.”
- “Hot and flushed.”

Q4. Urine?
A:
- “Barely peed today.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fainting + cold/clammy skin  
- Confusion  
- Fast breathing + dizziness  
- Very little pee  
- Severe chest/abdominal pain with faintness  

=======================================================
SECTION 2 — HYPOVOLEMIC SHOCK
=======================================================

CAUSES:
- Dehydration (vomiting/diarrhea)
- Severe burns
- Diabetes insipidus

CLUES:
- Dry mouth, extreme thirst
- Weakness
- Dizziness on standing
- Cold hands/feet
- History of fluid losses

Q1. Fluid loss?
A:
- “Been vomiting nonstop.”
- “Severe diarrhea for days.”

Q2. Thirst?
A:
- “Extremely thirsty.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion  
- Fast breathing  
- Inability to keep fluids down  

=======================================================
SECTION 3 — HEMORRHAGIC SHOCK
=======================================================

CAUSES:
- GI bleeding
- Trauma
- Postpartum bleeding
- Ruptured AAA
- Ruptured ectopic pregnancy

CLUES:
- Sudden weakness
- Dizziness/fainting
- Paleness
- Rapid heartbeat
- Black/tarry stools or vomiting blood
- Severe abdominal/pelvic pain

Q1. Bleeding?
A:
- “Black stools.”
- “Vomited blood.”
- “Heavy vaginal bleeding.”

Q2. Pain?
A:
- “Severe lower abdominal pain.”

RED FLAGS:
- Fainting + abdominal/back pain  
- Vomiting blood  
- Maroon/black stools  
- Heavy vaginal bleeding  

=======================================================
SECTION 4 — CARDIOGENIC SHOCK
=======================================================

CAUSES:
- Severe heart attack
- Advanced heart failure
- Arrhythmias
- Cardiomyopathy

CLUES:
- Severe shortness of breath
- Chest pain or heaviness
- Palpitations
- Cold/clammy skin
- Swelling
- History of CHF/heart disease

Q1. Chest symptoms?
A:
- “Heavy pressure in chest.”

Q2. Breathing?
A:
- “Can’t catch my breath.”

RED FLAGS:
- Chest pain + fainting  
- Pink frothy sputum  
- Blue lips/fingers  

=======================================================
SECTION 5 — OBSTRUCTIVE SHOCK
=======================================================

A. **PULMONARY EMBOLISM (massive)**
- Sudden SOB
- Chest pain
- Fainting
- Cold/clammy skin

B. **TAMPONADE**
- Weakness
- Trouble lying flat
- Chest pressure
- Dizziness

C. **TENSION PNEUMOTHORAX**
- Sudden chest pain
- Severe SOB
- Anxiety
- Collapsed lung history-level

Q1. Sudden breathing changes?
A:
- “Felt like I couldn’t breathe out of nowhere.”

RED FLAGS:
- Sudden SOB + fainting  
- Chest pain + cold/clammy skin  
- Trouble lying flat + dizziness  

=======================================================
SECTION 6 — DISTRIBUTIVE SHOCK (SEPSIS / ANAPHYLAXIS)
=======================================================

A. **SEPSIS**
- Fever or very low temperature
- Confusion
- Fast breathing
- Fast HR
- Weakness
- Recent infection/illness

Q1. Infection?
A:
- “Had fever/cough for days.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion + fever  
- Rapid breathing  
- Very low BP symptoms  

B. **ANAPHYLAXIS**
- Hives
- Throat tightness
- Wheezing
- Swelling of lips/tongue
- Vomiting

Q1. New food/med/exposure?
A:
- “Ate peanuts / new antibiotic.”

RED FLAGS:
- Trouble breathing  
- Throat closing  
- Swollen lips/tongue  

=======================================================
SECTION 7 — NEUROGENIC SHOCK
=======================================================

CAUSES:
- Spinal cord injury
- Trauma

CLUES:
- Warm, dry skin
- Low heart rate
- Dizziness
- Weakness/paralysis

Q1. Recent trauma?
A:
- “Fell and hit my back.”

RED FLAGS:
- Weakness below injury  
- Loss of bowel/bladder control  

=======================================================
SECTION 8 — ADRENAL CRISIS “SHOCK”
=======================================================

CAUSES:
- Addison’s
- Steroid withdrawal
- Severe infection in adrenal-insufficient patient

CLUES:
- Severe fatigue
- Nausea/vomiting
- Abdominal pain
- Salt craving
- Dizziness on standing
- Darkened skin (history-level)

Q1. Steroid use?
A:
- “Stopped steroids recently.”

Q2. Nausea/vomiting?
A:
- “Can’t keep anything down.”

RED FLAGS:
- Severe vomiting + dizziness  
- Confusion  
- Very low energy + fainting  

=======================================================
SECTION 9 — MIXED/UNDIFFERENTIATED SHOCK
=======================================================

CLUES:
- Severe illness with multiple organ symptoms
- Unclear cause
- Very low energy
- Confusion
- Cold/clammy or flushed skin
- Fast breathing

Q1. What changed suddenly?
A:
- “Suddenly felt extremely weak.”

RED FLAGS:
- Confusion  
- Severe SOB  
- Rapid heart rate  
- Not urinating  

=======================================================
SECTION 10 — SHOCK TRIAGE LOGIC FOR HEALTHYODA
=======================================================

ASK:
1. Chest pain?
2. Shortness of breath?
3. Abdominal pain?
4. Bleeding?
5. Fever?
6. Trauma?
7. Leg swelling?
8. Vomiting/diarrhea?
9. Allergic reaction?
10. Recent illness?

ROUTE:
- Chest pain + fainting → cardiogenic or dissection  
- Sudden SOB + fainting → massive PE  
- Abdominal pain + fainting → ruptured AAA / hemorrhage  
- Fever + confusion → septic shock  
- Hives + trouble breathing → anaphylaxis  
- Trauma + paralysis → neurogenic shock  
- Vomiting/diarrhea + dizziness → hypovolemic  
- Stopped steroids + dizziness → adrenal crisis  

=======================================================
SECTION 11 — GLOBAL SHOCK RED FLAGS (PATIENT-LEVEL)
=======================================================
- Fainting or near-fainting  
- Confusion  
- Cold, sweaty, grayish skin  
- Severe shortness of breath  
- Chest pain  
- Rapid heartbeat  
- Weak pulse  
- Barely urinating  
- Severe abdominal/back pain  
- Sudden leg swelling + SOB  
- Seizures or unresponsiveness
