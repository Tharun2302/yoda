SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL SLEEP HPI STRUCTURE
=========================================================

Q1. What is the main problem with your sleep?
A:
- "Can't fall asleep."
- "Wake up a lot."
- "Always tired during the day."
- "My partner says I stop breathing."

Q2. Duration of problem?
A:
- "Weeks."
- "Months."
- "Years."

Q3. Sleep schedule (weekdays vs weekends)?
A:
- "Bed at 11 pm, wake at 6 am."
- "Very irregular schedule."

Q4. Sleep environment?
A:
- "TV/phone in bed."
- "Noisy/bright bedroom."

Q5. Caffeine/alcohol/nicotine use?
A:
- "Several coffees in afternoon."
- "Alcohol to fall asleep."

Q6. Medical/psychiatric conditions?
A:
- "Anxiety."
- "Depression."
- "Chronic pain."

GLOBAL RED FLAGS:
- Falling asleep while driving
- Witnessed apneas with choking/gasping
- Violent movements during sleep with injury
- Sudden muscle weakness with emotions (cataplexy)
- New parasomnias in older adults (possible neurodegeneration)

=========================================================
SECTION 2 — INSOMNIA
=========================================================

TYPES:
- Sleep-onset insomnia (difficulty falling asleep)
- Sleep-maintenance insomnia (frequent awakenings)
- Early-morning awakening
- Mixed pattern

Q1. Which is hardest: falling asleep, staying asleep, or both?
A:
- "Takes 2 hours to fall asleep."
- "Wake up every 2 hours."
- "Wake up at 4 am and can't go back to sleep."

Q2. Approximate time to fall asleep?
A:
- "Usually more than an hour."

Q3. How many times do you wake at night?
A:
- "3–4 times."

Q4. Do you feel rested on waking?
A:
- "Never feel rested."

Q5. Daytime effects?
A:
- "Very tired at work."
- "Irritable."

Q6. Bedtime routine?
A:
- "On phone until I fall asleep."
- "TV on in background."

Q7. Worries or racing thoughts at night?
A:
- "Mind won't shut off."

INSOMNIA RED FLAGS:
- Insomnia + severe depression / suicidal ideation
- New onset insomnia with weight loss, fevers, or pain
- Insomnia + loud snoring/apneas (screen for OSA)

SECTION 4 — OBSTRUCTIVE SLEEP APNEA (OSA)
=========================================================

KEY SYMPTOMS:
- Loud snoring
- Witnessed apneas
- Gasping/choking at night
- Morning headaches
- Dry mouth on waking
- Daytime sleepiness

Q1. Does anyone say you snore or stop breathing in sleep?
A:
- "Yes, partner says I choke or stop breathing."

Q2. Do you wake up gasping or choking?
A:
- "Yes a few times a week."

Q3. Morning headaches?
A:
- "Often have dull headache."

Q4. Daytime sleepiness?
A:
- "Can fall asleep easily in the afternoon."

Q5. BMI / neck size pattern (history-level)?
A:
- "Overweight, large neck."

OSA RED FLAGS:
- Falling asleep while driving
- Severe daytime sleepiness
- Witnessed long pauses in breathing
- OSA symptoms in patient with heart disease or stroke history

SECTION 6 — RESTLESS LEGS SYNDROME (RLS)
=========================================================

KEY FEATURES:
- Urge to move legs, usually with unpleasant sensations
- Worse at rest/in the evening
- Relieved by movement
- Interferes with sleep onset

Q1. Any uncomfortable urges to move your legs at night?
A:
- "Yes, legs feel creepy-crawly."

Q2. When is it worst?
A:
- "In the evening when sitting or lying."

Q3. Does moving your legs help?
A:
- "Yes, walking around helps."

Q4. Family history?
A:
- "Mother has similar symptoms."

RLS RED FLAGS (underlying causes):
- RLS + anemia symptoms (iron deficiency)
- RLS + kidney disease
- RLS beginning suddenly with neurologic symptoms

=========================================================

Q1. Has anyone noticed you kick or jerk during sleep?
A:
- "Partner says I kick all night."

Q2. Do you wake feeling unrefreshed?

RED FLAGS:

SECTION 8 — PARASOMNIAS (NREM & REM)
=========================================================

NREM PARASOMNIAS:
- Sleepwalking
- Sleep terrors
- Confusional arousals

REM PARASOMNIA:
- REM behavior disorder (acting out dreams)

SLEEPWALKING:
Q1. Do you get out of bed and walk or do things in your sleep?
A:
- "Yes, found in kitchen with no memory."

Q2. Easy or hard to wake during episodes?
A:
- "Very hard to wake."

SLEEP TERRORS:
Q1. Episodes of screaming in sleep?
A:
- "Children: sit up screaming, inconsolable."

REM BEHAVIOR DISORDER:
Q1. Acting out dreams?
A:
- "Punching, kicking with dreams of being attacked."

PARASOMNIA RED FLAGS:
- Behaviors causing self-injury or injury to others
- New REM behavior disorder in older adults (possible neurodegenerative disease)
- Parasomnia + daytime neurologic symptoms

=========================================================
SECTION 9 — NARCOLEPSY / SUDDEN SLEEP ATTACKS
=========================================================

CORE FEATURES:
- Excessive daytime sleepiness
- Sleep attacks
- Cataplexy (sudden loss of muscle tone with emotion)
- Sleep paralysis
- Hypnagogic hallucinations

Q1. Do you suddenly fall asleep during the day despite adequate sleep?
A:
- "Yes, fall asleep mid-conversation sometimes."

Q2. Sudden muscle weakness when laughing or emotional?
A:
- "Knees buckle when I laugh hard."

Q3. Sleep paralysis?
A:
- "Wake up unable to move for a few seconds."

Q4. Vivid hallucinations as falling asleep or waking?
A:
- "See things in the room that aren’t there as I fall asleep."

NARCOLEPSY RED FLAGS:
- Sleep attacks in dangerous situations
- Cataplexy symptoms
- Sleep paralysis with severe fear/distress

=========================================================
SECTION 10 — CIRCADIAN RHYTHM DISORDERS
=========================================================

Delayed Sleep Phase:
- Difficulty falling asleep until very late (e.g., 2–3 am)
- Difficulty waking for obligations, but can sleep normally if schedule is free

Q1. Typical bedtime and wake time if no obligations?
A:
- "Fall asleep at 3 am, wake at 11 am."

Q2. Functioning when allowed to follow natural schedule?
A:
- "Feel fine on my own schedule."

Shift-Work Disorder:
Q1. Work schedule?
A:
- "Night shifts / rotating shifts."

Q2. Symptoms?
A:
- "Exhausted during shifts and can’t sleep during the day."

CIRCADIAN RED FLAGS:
- Falling asleep while driving to/from work
- Severe performance impairment

=========================================================
SECTION 11 — NIGHTMARES VS NIGHT TERRORS
=========================================================

NIGHTMARES:
- Vivid, disturbing dreams
- Fully awake and oriented upon waking
- Occur in later half of night (REM)

Q1. Recall of dream?
A:
- "Yes, remember the frightening dream."

NIGHT TERRORS:
- Usually in children
- Intense screaming, autonomic signs
- No recall of episode

RED FLAGS:
- Frequent nightmares + PTSD history
- Night terrors in adults

SLEEP DISORDER GLOBAL RED FLAGS
=========================================================
- Falling asleep while driving or in unsafe situations
- Witnessed apneas with choking/gasping
- Severe snoring + daytime sleepiness
- Violent sleep behaviors causing injury
- Sudden muscle weakness with emotion (cataplexy)
- New REM behavior disorder (acting out dreams) in older adults
- Insomnia with severe depression or suicidal thoughts
- Unexplained rapid weight gain + OSA symptoms
- New-onset parasomnias with neurologic signs
