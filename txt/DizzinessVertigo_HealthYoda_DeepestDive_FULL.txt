SECTION 1 — WHAT TYPE OF “DIZZINESS”?
===========================================================

VERTIGO = spinning sensation.
PRESYNCOPE = feeling faint.
IMBALANCE = off-balance, unsteady.
NON-SPECIFIC = can’t describe, floating.

Q1. What does it feel like?
A:
- “Room spinning.” → TRUE VERTIGO
- “Feel like I’ll pass out.” → PRESYNCOPE
- “Unsteady on feet.” → IMBALANCE
- “Floating sensation.” → NON-SPECIFIC

Q2. Triggers?
A:
- “Turning my head.”
- “Standing up.”

RED FLAGS:
- Difficulty walking  
- Falling to one side  
- Trouble speaking

SECTION 2 — TRUE VERTIGO (PERIPHERAL VS CENTRAL)
===========================================================

PERIPHERAL (inner ear):
- Sudden onset
- Intense spinning
- Nausea/vomiting
- Worse with head movement
- Hearing symptoms (sometimes)

CENTRAL (brainstem/cerebellum):
- Mild to moderate spinning
- Severe imbalance
- Neurologic symptoms (double vision, numbness)
- Headache

Q1. Head movement worsen symptoms?
A:
- “Yes, turning head makes it worse.” (Peripheral)

Q2. Neurologic symptoms?
A:
- “Double vision.” (Central)
- “Numbness/weakness.” (Central)

RED FLAGS:
- Severe imbalance unable to walk  
- Double vision  
- Weakness or numbness  
- New headache + vertigo  

===========================================================
SECTION 3 — BPPV (Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo)
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Sudden vertigo lasting seconds
- Triggered by rolling in bed, looking up
- No hearing loss
- No ear fullness

Q1. What triggers dizziness?
A:
- “Rolling over in bed.”
- “Looking up.”

Q2. Duration?
A:
- “Only lasts a few seconds.”

RED FLAGS:
- None typical unless atypical symptoms present  

===========================================================
SECTION 4 — VESTIBULAR NEURITIS / LABYRINTHITIS
===========================================================

VESTIBULAR NEURITIS:
- Sudden, severe vertigo lasting hours–days
- Nausea/vomiting
- Usually after viral illness
- No hearing loss

LABYRINTHITIS:
- Same as neuritis + hearing loss

Q1. Duration?
A:
- “Hours to days.”

Q2. Recent illness?
A:
- “Had a cold last week.”

Q3. Hearing loss?
A:
- “Yes, muffled.” (Labyrinthitis)

RED FLAGS:
- Persistent vomiting  
- Severe imbalance  

===========================================================
SECTION 5 — MÉNIÈRE’S DISEASE
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Episodic vertigo (20 min – hours)
- Hearing loss (fluctuating)
- Tinnitus (ringing)
- Ear fullness/pressure

Q1. Ringing in ears?
A:
- “Yes, loud ringing.”

Q2. Hearing problems?
A:
- “Comes and goes.”

RED FLAGS:
- Progressive hearing loss  

===========================================================
SECTION 6 — ACOUSTIC NEUROMA (VESTIBULAR SCHWANNOMA)
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Gradually progressive one-sided hearing loss
- Persistent imbalance
- Tinnitus
- Mild chronic dizziness (not episodic)
- Facial numbness (late)

Q1. Hearing?
A:
- “Left ear hearing worse over months.”

RED FLAGS:
- Slowly progressive one-sided hearing loss  
- Facial numbness  

===========================================================
SECTION 7 — CENTRAL VERTIGO (STROKE, MS, TUMOR)
===========================================================

STROKE-LEVEL CLUES:
- Sudden vertigo
- Severe imbalance
- Can’t walk or stand
- Double vision
- Slurred speech
- Weakness
- New severe headache
- Age >60 or vascular risk

MS/DEMYELINATION:
- Episodic vertigo
- Eye movement abnormalities
- Numbness/tingling

TUMOR/CEREBELLAR LESIONS:
- Progressive imbalance
- Headache
- Nausea/vomiting

Q1. Any vision problems?
A:
- “Double vision.”

Q2. Balance?
A:
- “Can’t walk straight.”

RED FLAGS:
- Inability to walk or stand  
- Weakness  
- Double vision  
- Slurred speech  
- Sudden severe headache  

===========================================================
SECTION 8 — CARDIAC/PRESYNCOPE DIZZINESS
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Lightheadedness
- Tunnel vision
- Palpitations
- Chest discomfort
- Occurs with exertion
- History of heart issues

Q1. Did you fully pass out?
A:
- “Almost passed out.”

Q2. Palpitations?
A:
- “Heart suddenly racing.”

RED FLAGS:
- Chest pain  
- Syncope with exertion  

===========================================================
SECTION 9 — ANEMIA / METABOLIC DIZZINESS
===========================================================

ANEMIA:
- Fatigue
- Pallor
- Weakness
- Heavy menstrual bleeding
- Black stools

METABOLIC:
- Low blood sugar
- Dehydration
- Electrolyte imbalance

Q1. Bleeding?
A:
- “Heavy periods.”
- “Black stools.”

Q2. Eating?
A:
- “Skipped meals.”

RED FLAGS:
- Black stools  
- Severe vomiting/diarrhea  

===========================================================
SECTION 10 — ORTHOSTATIC DIZZINESS
===========================================================

CLUES:
- Happens on standing
- Dizziness improves when lying down
- Dehydration
- Medications (BP meds, diuretics)
- Blood loss

Q1. Position?
A:
- “Got dizzy when standing up.”

RED FLAGS:
- Standing dizziness + fainting  
- Standing dizziness + black stools  

===========================================================
SECTION 11 — PSYCHIATRIC / FUNCTIONAL DIZZINESS
===========================================================

ANXIETY:
- Lightheadedness
- Tingling
- Feeling detached

PERSISTENT POSTURAL-PERCEPTUAL DIZZINESS (PPPD):
- Chronic rocking sensation
- Worse with visual motion

Q1. Any panic or anxiety?
A:
- “Yes, episodes of panic.”

===========================================================
SECTION 12 — GLOBAL DIZZINESS & VERTIGO RED FLAGS
===========================================================
- Inability to walk or stand  
- Double vision  
- Weakness or numbness  
- Slurred speech  
- Severe headache  
- Hearing loss in one ear  
- Chest pain  
- Palpitations  
- Fainting  
- Black stools  
- Vomiting blood  
- Fever + neck stiffness  
- Sudden, severe vertigo with neurologic symptoms  

===========================================================
SECTION 13 — HEALTHYODA DIZZINESS TRIAGE LOGIC
===========================================================

ASK:
1. Spinning or faint feeling?
2. Can you walk?
3. Any weakness/numbness?
4. Double vision or speech problems?
5. Hearing loss or ringing?
6. Chest pain or palpitations?
7. Head movement worsen symptoms?
8. Any fever, neck pain/stiffness?
9. Position relationship (standing/sitting/lying)?
10. Recent illness or ear symptoms?

ROUTE:
- Spinning + head movement triggers → BPPV  
- Spinning + hearing loss → labyrinthitis/Ménière’s  
- Sudden vertigo + imbalance → central vertigo / stroke  
- Dizziness + chest pain/palpitations → cardiac  
- Dizziness + standing → orthostatic  
- Dizziness + black stools → anemia  
- Dizziness + fever + neck stiffness → meningitis  
- Gradual hearing loss + imbalance → acoustic neuroma
