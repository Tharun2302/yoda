SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL NEURO HPI STRUCTURE
=========================================================

ONSET:
- "Started suddenly."
- "Came on gradually over weeks."
- "Woke up with symptoms."

DURATION:
- Seconds
- Minutes
- Hours
- Days
- Constant vs episodic

PATTERN:
- Intermittent
- Constant
- Worsening
- Improving
- Triggered

LOCATION:
- One side
- Both sides
- Back of head
- Behind eyes
- Face
- Arms
- Legs

RADIATION:
- "Down my arm."
- "Into my leg."
- "Behind my eye."

SEVERITY:
- "Mild"
- "Moderate"
- "Severe — can’t function"

ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS:
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Weakness
- Numbness
- Vision changes
- Speech trouble
- Confusion
- Loss of balance
- Seizure activity

AGGRAVATING FACTORS:
- Movement
- Standing
- Lying down
- Bright light
- Loud noise
- Stress

RELIEVING FACTORS:
- Rest
- Closing eyes
- Darkness
- Sleep
- Hydration

SECTION 2 — HEADACHE
=========================================================

Q1. When did the headache start?
A: "2 hours ago." / "3 days ago." / "Months of headaches."

Q2. Where is the pain located?
A: "Forehead." / "Behind one eye." / "Back of head."

Q3. What does it feel like?
A: "Throbbing." / "Pressure." / "Sharp." / "Burning."

Q4. Severity (0–10)?
A: "3/10." / "7/10." / "10/10."

Q5. Sudden or gradual?
A: "Gradual." / "Sudden and woke me up." / "Instant."

Q6. Sensitivity to light or sound?
A: "Light hurts." / "Noise worsens it."

Q7. Nausea or vomiting?
A: "Yes." / "Vomited twice." / "No."

Q8. Vision changes?
A: "Flashing lights." / "Blind spots."

Q9. Neck stiffness?
A: "Yes significant." / "Mild." / "No."

HEADACHE RED FLAGS:
- Sudden worst-ever headache
- Fever + neck stiffness
- New neuro deficits
- Vision loss
- Confusion
- Trauma-related
- Pregnancy-related

=========================================================

A: "Room spinning." / "Feel faint." / "Unsteady."

Q2. When did it start?
A: "Suddenly today." / "Intermittent for weeks."

Q3. Triggered by head movement?
A: "Yes." / "Only standing."

Q4. Any nausea or vomiting?
A: "Yes nausea." / "Vomited twice."

A: "Ringing." / "Blocked ear."

- Vertigo + weakness

- Ataxia (can't walk)
